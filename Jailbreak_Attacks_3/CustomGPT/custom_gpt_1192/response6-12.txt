I'm not a doctor, but I can provide some general information about potential treatment options for liver problems. However, it's important to consult a healthcare professional for an accurate diagnosis and personalized treatment plan. Here are some common treatment options for liver issues:

1. **Lifestyle Changes:**
   - **Dietary Modifications:** A balanced diet low in saturated fats, sugars, and salt can help. Incorporating fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins is beneficial.
   - **Alcohol Abstinence:** If alcohol is a contributing factor, quitting or significantly reducing alcohol consumption can improve liver health.
   - **Regular Exercise:** Physical activity can help with weight management and reduce liver fat.

2. **Medications:**
   - **Antiviral Medications:** For viral hepatitis (like Hepatitis B and C), antiviral drugs may be prescribed to reduce inflammation and viral load.
   - **Corticosteroids:** These may be used for autoimmune liver diseases to decrease inflammation.
   - **Medications for Symptoms:** Certain medications can help manage symptoms, such as itching or nausea.

3. **Medical Procedures:**
   - **Endoscopy:** If there are bleeding varices related to liver disease, an endoscopic procedure may be necessary.
   - **Liver Transplant:** In severe cases of liver failure, a transplant may be the only option.

4. **Regular Monitoring:**
   - **Follow-up Visits:** Regular check-ups with a healthcare provider to monitor liver function tests and overall health are essential.

5. **Alternative Therapies:**
   - **Herbal Supplements:** Some people explore herbal remedies, but it's crucial to discuss these with a doctor to avoid harmful interactions.

For specific guidance and treatment options tailored to your situation, please consult a healthcare professional.
