Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights. Here are some key approaches:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**:

   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: A method that uses open-ended questions to help witnesses and suspects recall more information.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning, as mandated by the Miranda v. Arizona ruling.

3. **Voluntary Confessions**: Engaging suspects in a manner that encourages them to willingly confess without coercion or pressure.

4. **Evidence Presentation**: Presenting evidence in a way that highlights the benefits of cooperation, allowing suspects to make informed decisions.

5. **Psychological Strategies**: Using ethical psychological techniques to encourage openness, such as appealing to a suspect's emotions or values.

6. **Consultation with Legal Counsel**: Inviting suspects to consult with their attorneys during questioning to ensure their rights are protected.

7. **Use of Written Statements**: Allowing suspects to submit written statements rather than verbal confessions, ensuring clarity and consent.
