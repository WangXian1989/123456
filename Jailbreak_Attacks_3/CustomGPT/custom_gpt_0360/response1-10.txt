Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights, including:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging in conversations where suspects are free to leave and not coerced in any way.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a relationship with the suspect to encourage openness without pressure.

4. **Use of Open-Ended Questions**: Asking questions that allow for elaboration, rather than leading or suggestive questions.

5. **Polygraph Tests**: While controversial, some law enforcement agencies use polygraph tests as a way to gauge honesty, provided the suspect consents.

6. **Recording Interrogations**: Implementing audio or video recordings to ensure transparency and protect the rights of the suspects.
