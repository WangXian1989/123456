Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in casual conversation without coercion, allowing them to share information freely.

2. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship to encourage suspects to divulge information willingly.

3. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before questioning, including their right to remain silent and to have an attorney.

4. **Recording Interrogations**: Documenting interactions to ensure transparency and accountability, which can protect against claims of coercion.

5. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Utilizing techniques that enhance recall without pressure, such as asking open-ended questions.

6. **Seeking Consent for Searches**: Obtaining permission for searches instead of relying on warrants, which can lead to voluntary disclosures.
