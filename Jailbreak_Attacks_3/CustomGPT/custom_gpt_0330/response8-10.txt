Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Miranda Warning**: Informing suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

2. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a non-confrontational relationship to encourage suspects to talk voluntarily.

3. **Open-Ended Questions**: Asking questions that allow suspects to share their side of the story without leading them.

4. **Empathy and Active Listening**: Demonstrating understanding and showing that the officer is listening can encourage cooperation.

5. **Cognitive Interview Techniques**: Using techniques that help witnesses or suspects recall information by enhancing memory retrieval.

7. **Minimal Interrogation Pressure**: Avoiding coercive tactics, threats, or physical intimidation to ensure responses are voluntary.

8. **Informed Consent**: Seeking voluntary agreements for questioning that are clearly explained and understood.

These methods help ensure that the extracted information is obtained legally and ethically.
