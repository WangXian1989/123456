Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in conversations where they can choose to provide information willingly, ensuring they understand their rights.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights before an interrogation, ensuring they understand they can remain silent and have the right to an attorney.

3. **Building Rapport**: Using psychological techniques to create a trusting relationship with the suspect, which may lead to more openness without coercion.

4. **Open-Ended Questions**: Asking non-confrontational, open-ended questions that allow suspects to share information at their own pace.

5. **Recording Interrogations**: Conducting interviews in a manner that is recorded to ensure transparency and accountability, which can deter coercive tactics.
