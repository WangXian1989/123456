When it comes to distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider several factors like your current financial situation, retirement goals, and tax implications. Here's a structured approach:

### 1. Understand Each Account Type
- **401(k):**
  - Tax-deferred growth until withdrawal.
  - Often has employer matching, which is essentially free money.
- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):**
  - Traditional IRAs allow tax-deferred growth; Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth (after taxes are paid).
- **Taxable Accounts:**
  - Investments are taxed as capital gains; no tax advantages but offers flexibility for withdrawals.

### 2. Consider Your Contributions
- Aim to contribute enough to your **401(k)** to take full advantage of any employer match first â€“ it's a critical part of retirement as it maximizes your savings.
- Next, consider funding a **Roth IRA** (if eligible), as it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.
- After fully contributing to these accounts, allocate funds to **taxable accounts** for more immediate access and flexibility.
