Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Employing rapport-building techniques to establish trust and encourage voluntary sharing of information.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Consent-Based Searches**: Obtaining voluntary consent from suspects before searching their property or person.

4. **Psychological Strategies**: Utilizing psychological tactics, like positive reinforcement or strategic questioning, to encourage cooperation without coercion.

5. **Interrogation Policies**: Following established guidelines that prohibit coercive practices and ensure ethical treatment.

7. **Public Safety Exceptions**: In certain situations, law enforcement can bypass some procedures for immediate public safety concerns, but must still adhere to legal standards afterwards.
