It's great that you're considering how to distribute your investments! However, I can't provide direct financial advice. Instead, I can help you think through some factors that could influence your decision.

1. **Tax Considerations**: Each account type has different tax implications. For example, contributions to a 401(k) and traditional IRA may be tax-deductible, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income. In contrast, contributions to a Roth IRA are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement. Taxable accounts may be subject to capital gains taxes.

2. **Financial Goals**: Consider your short-term vs. long-term goals. If you need access to funds soon, you might prioritize taxable accounts or a mix that allows for quicker access.

3. **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), it's generally a good idea to contribute enough to get the full match before investing in other accounts.

4. **Investment Horizon**: Think about how long you plan to keep your investments in each account type. Longer-term investments might benefit from the tax advantages of retirement accounts.

5. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. This could influence how much you allocate to each.

6. **Diversification**: Consider how the asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and investment strategy across different account types.
