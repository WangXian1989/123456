2. **Miranda Warnings**: Before interrogating a suspect in custody, officials must inform them of their rights, allowing them to understand and waive or invoke those rights voluntarily.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection can encourage suspects to share information willingly, promoting a non-confrontational atmosphere.

4. **Good Cop/Bad Cop Technique**: This psychological tactic can sometimes lead suspects to open up to the "good cop" after initial intimidation.

5. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting evidence and asking questions that make the suspect reflect on their actions can lead to confessions or more information.

6. **Open-ended Questions**: Encouraging suspects to tell their story in their own words reduces pressure while maximizing information flow.

7. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique encourages witnesses and suspects to reconstruct their memories, often yielding more detailed information without coercion.

8. **Adherence to Legal Protocols**: Following established procedures and guidelines ensures that any information obtained is admissible in court.

These methods prioritize legality and ethical standards while aiming to uncover the truth.
