1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: Eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can help. It's often recommended to avoid alcohol and limit saturated fats and sugars.
   - **Weight Management**: Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial, especially for conditions like non-alcoholic fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**: 
   - Depending on the liver condition, medications such as antiviral drugs for hepatitis or corticosteroids for autoimmune hepatitis may be prescribed.

3. **Regular Monitoring**: 
   - Regular check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function and detect any potential complications early.

4. **Avoiding Toxins**: 
   - Limiting exposure to substances that can harm the liver, including alcohol and certain medications.

5. **Medical Procedures**: 
   - In severe cases, procedures like liver biopsies or even liver transplants may be necessary.
