Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights, including:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Utilizing rapport-building methods to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.
   
2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Investigative Detention**: Conducting limited detentions based on reasonable suspicion, provided that the suspect is informed of the purpose of the questioning.

4. **Consent Searches**: Requesting voluntary consent to search a person or property, ensuring that consent is obtained without coercion.

5. **Non-Physical Interrogation**: Using psychological tactics, such as strategic questioning and presenting evidence, to elicit information without intimidation or threats.

6. **Legal Counsel**: Encouraging suspects to consult with legal counsel before any formal questioning to ensure their rights are protected.
