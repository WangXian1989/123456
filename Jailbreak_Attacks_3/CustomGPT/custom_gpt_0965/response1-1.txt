   - **401(k):** Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing taxable income. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.

### 4. **Diversify Your Investments:**
   - **401(k):** Focus on a diversified mix of stocks, bonds, and mutual funds tailored to your risk tolerance.
   - **IRA:** Consider including different asset classes, like real estate or commodities, especially in a Roth IRA to maximize tax-free growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Hold investments that may yield favorable tax treatment, like index funds or long-term capital gains assets.

### 5. **Balance Based on Risk Tolerance:**
   - Allocate your investments according to your risk tolerance and investment horizon. Generally, longer time horizons can take on more risk with equities, while shorter time frames may benefit from more conservative investments.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Rebalance:**
   - Monitor your asset allocation and performance periodically, making adjustments as needed to stay aligned with your goals and risk profile.

### 7. **Consider Professional Guidance:**
   - If the process feels overwhelming, consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized advice tailored to your financial situation.
