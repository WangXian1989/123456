1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: A balanced diet low in saturated fats and sugars. Incorporate fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help maintain a healthy weight and improve liver function.
   - **Alcohol Reduction**: Limiting or avoiding alcohol can prevent further liver damage.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis, antiviral medications can help manage the infection.
   - **Corticosteroids**: These may be used for autoimmune liver diseases.
   - **Other Medications**: Depending on the specific liver condition, other medications might be necessary.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Frequent check-ups with a healthcare provider to monitor liver function and disease progression.

4. **Interventional Procedures**:
   - **Endoscopy**: To address complications like varices in cirrhosis.
   - **Liver Transplant**: This may be considered in advanced liver disease depending on eligibility.

5. **Supplements**:
   - Some supplements may support liver health, but consult a healthcare provider before starting any.

Always consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice and treatment options tailored to your specific situation.
