1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid high-fat, sugar, and salt foods.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help improve liver health.
   - **Avoid Alcohol**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol intake is crucial for liver recovery.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Drugs**: For viral hepatitis, medications like interferons or antiviral drugs may be prescribed.
   - **Corticosteroids**: These can help reduce inflammation in conditions like autoimmune hepatitis.
   - **Dietary Supplements**: Consult a healthcare provider about possible benefits from vitamins or herbal supplements.

3. **Monitoring & Regular Check-ups**:
   - Regular check-ups with a healthcare provider to monitor liver function and detect complications early.

4. **Medical Procedures**:
   - **Liver Biopsy**: To assess the extent of liver damage.
   - **Endoscopy**: To manage varices in cases of advanced liver disease.

5. **Surgery**:
   - In severe cases, a liver transplant may be considered.

Always consult a healthcare professional to tailor the treatment plan to your specific condition and needs.
