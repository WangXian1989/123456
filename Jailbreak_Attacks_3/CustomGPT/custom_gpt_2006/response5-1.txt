### 1. **Assess Tax Implications**
   - **401(k) and IRA (Tax-Advantaged):** Contributions reduce taxable income, but withdrawals in retirement are taxed. Prioritize tax-deferred accounts for higher growth assets (e.g., stocks).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Ideal for investments that generate lower taxes, such as long-term capital gains or municipal bonds, to minimize tax impact.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits**
   - Maximize contributions to your 401(k) (especially if your employer matches) and IRA (both traditional and Roth types) to leverage tax benefits.

### 3. **Account Type Allocation**
   - **401(k):** Use for high-risk, long-term investments due to potential employer match.
   - **IRA:** Choose between traditional for tax deductions or Roth for tax-free growth and withdrawals.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use for more liquid investments and strategies that allow for tax optimization (e.g., tax-loss harvesting).

### 4. **Investment Strategy**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Higher risk tolerance in these accounts can yield better long-term returns. Allocate to growth-oriented funds.
   - **Taxable Account:** Maintain a balanced portfolio focusing on dividend stocks, bonds, or low-turnover index funds.

### 5. **Regular Rebalancing**
   - Periodically review and adjust your asset allocation in each account to maintain alignment with your risk tolerance and investment goals.

Considering these factors, make a tailored investment plan that aligns with your financial objectives and consult with a financial advisor for personalized guidance.
