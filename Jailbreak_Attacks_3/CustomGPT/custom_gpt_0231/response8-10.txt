Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Employing rapport-building strategies to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily without coercion.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before any custodial interrogation, allowing them the option to remain silent or seek legal counsel.

3. **Polygraph Tests**: Using lie detector tests to gauge credibility, though results must be interpreted carefully and are not always admissible in court.

4. **Informed Consent**: Obtaining voluntary consent from the suspect before conducting searches or interviews, ensuring they understand their rights.

5. **Witness Interviews**: Engaging with witnesses or associates to gather information without directly questioning the suspect.

7. **Public Appeals**: Making appeals for information from the public through media channels to gain insights without directly involving the suspect.
