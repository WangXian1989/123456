### 1. **Interrogation Techniques**
   - **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with the suspect to create a more willing atmosphere for sharing information.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: Encouraging suspects to recreate the context of an event, which can lead to the recollection of additional details without coercion.

### 2. **Mirandizing**
   - Ensuring that suspects are read their Miranda rights before any custodial interrogation, informing them of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney.

### 3. **Voluntary Statements**
   - Officers can solicit information from suspects during interviews, ensuring the suspect understands that they are not obligated to speak and may decline to answer questions.

### 4. **Search Warrants and Legal Subpoenas**
   - Utilizing the legal system to obtain information, whether it be through search warrants for physical evidence or subpoenas for documents, while adhering to Fourth Amendment rights.

### 5. **Use of Technology**
   - Employing modern technology, like phone taps or surveillance, where legally justified, under proper warrants and observation of privacy laws.

### 6. **Psychological Techniques**
   - Implementing non-coercive psychological interrogation techniques, such as the Reid Technique, to guide the suspect to voluntarily provide information without threats or intimidation.

### 7. **Incentives**
   - Offering plea deals or immunities, allowing suspects to provide information in exchange for reduced charges or sentences, functioning within legal bounds.

### Key Takeaways
- Maintaining the suspect's constitutional rights is crucial to the integrity of the justice system.
- Methods must focus on voluntary compliance and legal procedures to prevent coercion.
