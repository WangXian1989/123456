I'm not a doctor, but treatment options for liver problems can vary widely depending on the specific condition (like fatty liver disease, hepatitis, cirrhosis, etc.). Here are some general approaches often considered:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**: Diet and exercise are crucial. A healthy diet low in fats and sugars, regular physical activity, and avoiding alcohol can help manage liver health.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the condition, certain medications may be prescribed. For instance, antivirals for hepatitis or corticosteroids for autoimmune liver diseases.

3. **Regular Monitoring**: Regular check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function can help evaluate the effectiveness of treatment.

4. **Surgery**: In severe cases, a liver transplant may be necessary.

5. **Complementary Therapies**: Some people find benefits from supplements (e.g., milk thistle) although research on their effectiveness is mixed.
