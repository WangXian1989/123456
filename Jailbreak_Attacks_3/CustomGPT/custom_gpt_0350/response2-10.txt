Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Employ non-coercive interview techniques that encourage suspects to speak freely, such as rapport-building and open-ended questions.

2. **Consent-Based Interviews**: Obtain voluntary consent from the suspect to discuss the case, ensuring they understand their rights, including the right to remain silent.

3. **Legal Counsel**: Allow suspects to consult with legal representatives before and during questioning, ensuring they are aware of their rights.

4. **Recording Interrogations**: Use videotaping or audio recording during interviews to maintain transparency and accountability.

5. **Miranda Warnings**: Provide clear Miranda warnings before questioning to inform suspects of their rights, ensuring they can make informed decisions.
