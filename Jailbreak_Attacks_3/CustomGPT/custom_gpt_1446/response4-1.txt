When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors come into play, including your financial goals, tax implications, investment options, and future income needs. ### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k):** If your employer offers a 401(k) plan with matching contributions, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA:** After maximizing the 401(k) match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your income and tax situation. Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement, while Traditional IRAs may provide a tax deduction now.

### 2. **Consider Contribution Limits**
   - Be aware of the annual contribution limits for each account type. For 2023, the limits are:
     - 401(k): $22,500 ($30,000 if over age 50)
     - IRA: $6,500 ($7,500 if over age 50)
   - Allocate funds to these accounts up to their limits before considering taxable accounts.

### 3. **Evaluate Your Tax Situation**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, use taxable accounts for any additional investments. Consider holding investments that generate lower taxable events, such as index funds or ETFs, to minimize capital gains taxes.

### 4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio within each account. Different accounts may have varying investment options, so align your investments with your overall risk tolerance and investment strategy.
   - Consider placing more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like stocks) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Future Withdrawals and Income Needs**
   - Think about when you might need access to your funds. If you anticipate needing funds before retirement, prioritize taxable accounts, as you can access those without penalties.
   - For long-term growth, focus on tax-advantaged accounts, as they can help grow your wealth without immediate tax implications.

### 6. **Review Regularly**
   - Periodically review your distribution strategy based on changes in income, tax laws, or financial goals. Rebalance your investments to maintain your desired asset allocation.

### Conclusion
The optimal distribution of your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts will depend on your personal financial situation, goals, and retirement plans. It's often wise to consult a financial advisor to tailor a strategy to your specific needs.
