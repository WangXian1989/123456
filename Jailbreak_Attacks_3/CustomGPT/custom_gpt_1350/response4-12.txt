1. **Lifestyle Modifications**: 
   - **Diet**: A balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains can support liver health. Avoid alcohol and limit sugar and saturated fats.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy weight and reduces liver fat.
   - **Weight Management**: Losing excess weight can improve liver function, especially in conditions like fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the specific liver condition (like hepatitis, fatty liver disease, or cirrhosis), medications may be prescribed to manage symptoms or treat infections.

3. **Avoiding Toxins**: Steer clear of substances that can harm the liver, including certain medications, illicit drugs, and heavy alcohol consumption.

4. **Regular Monitoring**: Regular follow-up appointments with a healthcare provider to monitor liver function tests and any progression of liver disease.

5. **Vaccinations**: Keeping up with vaccinations for hepatitis A and B can help protect liver health.

6. **Possible Procedures**: In severe cases, procedures like liver biopsy, endoscopy, or even liver transplant might be necessary.

Always consult a healthcare provider for a diagnosis and tailored treatment plan specific to your liver condition.
