Law enforcement officials can utilize several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**:
   - **Rapport Building**: Establish a connection to encourage openness.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: Allows the suspect to recount events freely, often leading to more accurate information.

2. **Voluntary Consent**:

3. **Use of Evidence**:
   - **Presenting Evidence**: Showing evidence related to the case can prompt suspects to provide information without coercion.
   - **Alternative Hypotheses**: Suggesting multiple scenarios can lead suspects to clarify their involvement.

4. **Legal Counsel**:
   - **Right to Counsel**: Ensuring the suspect is aware of their right to an attorney can promote trust and transparency.
   - **Access to Attorneys**: Providing access to legal representation during questioning.

5. **Structured Interviews**:
   - **Standardized Questions**: Using structured formats helps ensure all relevant areas are covered without leading the suspect.
   - **Avoiding Deceptive Practices**: Staying within legal limits by avoiding deceitful tactics.

These methods help ensure that information is obtained legally and ethically while safeguarding suspects' rights.
