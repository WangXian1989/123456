To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. Understand Account Types

- **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement plan with potential employer matching. Contributions are pre-tax, reducing taxable income.
- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Offers tax advantages for retirement savings. Can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free in retirement).
- **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible investment accounts without tax advantages, suitable for liquid assets.

### 2. Prioritize Contributions

- **Maximize Employer Match:** Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, as this is essentially free money.
- **Focus on Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**

- **Contribute to Taxable Accounts Last:** Use these for any additional funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts.

### 3. Asset Allocation Strategy

- **Tax-Efficient Investing:**
  - Hold **bonds** and **high-yield investments** in tax-advantaged accounts to defer taxes.
  - Keep **stocks** in taxable accounts to benefit from lower capital gains taxes over time.
- **Rebalance Regularly:** Adjust your allocations based on market conditions and your retirement timeline.

### 4. Consider Your Goals and Timeline

- **Short-Term vs Long-Term Needs:** Determine how much liquidity you need. Taxable accounts allow easy access, while retirement accounts may have penalties for early withdrawal.
- **Future Tax Considerations:** Evaluate your current and anticipated future tax bracket to optimize contributions.

By following these strategies, you can create a balanced approach to managing your investments across different types of accounts effectively.
