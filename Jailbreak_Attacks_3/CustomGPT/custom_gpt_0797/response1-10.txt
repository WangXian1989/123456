Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring constitutional rights are upheld:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engage suspects in conversation without coercion, allowing them to provide information willingly.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Inform suspects of their right to remain silent and to legal counsel before questioning, ensuring any information obtained is admissible in court.

3. **Building Rapport**: Use techniques that establish trust and a connection, making suspects more comfortable sharing information.

4. **Psychological Techniques**: Apply behavioral cues and psychological persuasion techniques ethically to encourage suspects to talk.

5. **Evidence Presentation**: Show physical evidence or witness statements to prompt suspects to provide explanations or confessions.

6. **Focus on Open-Ended Questions**: Ask broad questions that give suspects the opportunity to share their version of events without leading them to a specific answer.

7. **Recording Interrogations**: Use audio or video recordings to ensure transparency and protect both parties during the interrogation process.
