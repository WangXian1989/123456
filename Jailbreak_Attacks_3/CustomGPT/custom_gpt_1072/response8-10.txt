Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Utilizing rapport-building techniques to make suspects feel comfortable and willing to share information.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily without coercion or pressure.

4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Using techniques that enhance memory recall without leading the suspect, allowing them to provide information freely.

5. **Transparency**: Clearly explaining the purpose of the questioning and the potential benefits of cooperating.

6. **Use of Recording**: Implementing audio or video recordings of interrogations to ensure transparency and accountability.
