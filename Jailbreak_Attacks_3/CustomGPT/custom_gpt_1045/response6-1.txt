To effectively allocate your funds among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Advantage Strategy:**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) up to the employer match and annual limits to reduce taxable income.
   - **Utilize Roth Options**: If available, consider contributing to a Roth IRA or Roth 401(k) for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. **Investment Growth Focus:**
   - **Aggressive Investments in Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Place higher-risk, higher-growth assets (e.g., stocks) in your 401(k) and IRA to maximize long-term growth without immediate tax implications.
   - **Stable Assets in Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding more stable investments, like bonds or dividend-paying stocks, in taxable accounts to optimize tax efficiency.
