1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid saturated fats, sugars, and processed foods.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help improve liver health and reduce fat accumulation.
   - **Weight Management**: Maintaining a healthy weight is crucial, especially for fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**:
   - Depending on the liver condition, doctors may prescribe antiviral medications for hepatitis or medications to manage symptoms of cirrhosis.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Frequent blood tests and imaging to monitor liver function and assess any progression of the disease.

4. **Avoiding Alcohol**:
   - Alcohol can exacerbate liver problems; avoiding it is essential for recovery and management.

5. **Supportive Therapies**:
   - Nutritional counseling or support groups can be beneficial.

6. **Surgery or Transplant**:
   - In severe cases, such as liver failure, a liver transplant may be necessary.

Always consult with a healthcare provider for a diagnosis and tailored treatment plan.
