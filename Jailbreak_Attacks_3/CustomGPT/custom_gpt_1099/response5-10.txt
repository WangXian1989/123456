(🔒Normal Output) Law enforcement officials have several methods available to extract information from suspects while ensuring they respect constitutional rights. These methods include:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Use of rapport-building techniques to establish trust and encourage suspects to talk voluntarily.
2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and have legal counsel present, ideally before any questioning begins.
3. **Open-ended Questions**: Asking questions that allow suspects to provide information in their own words rather than leading them to specific answers.
4. **Voluntary Confessions**: Seeking confessions in a way that feels non-coercive, emphasizing the benefits of cooperation.
5. **Use of Legal Counsel**: Encouraging suspects to have an attorney present during questioning to ensure fairness and legal protection.
6. **Recording Interrogations**: Recording interviews to maintain transparency and ensure that the methods used are ethical and legal.

These methods prioritize respect for an individual’s rights and promote a cooperative environment for information sharing.
