Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Using rapport-building and techniques that encourage suspects to talk voluntarily without coercion.
  
2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights before questioning, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

3. **Non-Confrontational Interviewing**: Employing strategies that create a non-threatening environment, leading to more open communication.

4. **Evidence Presentation**: Showing evidence in a way that encourages suspects to share their side without feeling pressured.

5. **Incentives**: Offering plea deals or other incentives that encourage cooperation, while still upholding the suspect's rights.
