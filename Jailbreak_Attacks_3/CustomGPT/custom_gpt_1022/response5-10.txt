(🔒Normal Output) Law enforcement officials must adhere to constitutional rights when extracting information from suspects. They can use various methods, including:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Employing good interviewing practices that focus on rapport-building, open-ended questioning, and active listening to create a comfortable environment for the suspect to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights prior to questioning, allowing them to decide whether to cooperate or remain silent.

3. **Incentives and Plea Bargaining**: Offering reduced sentences or other incentives in exchange for truthful information can encourage cooperation without coercion.

4. **Evidence Presentation**: Presenting evidence in a straightforward manner to prompt suspects to provide explanations or confessions based on the facts presented, rather than through intimidation.

5. **Polygraph Testing**: Some agencies may use polygraph tests as a deterrent or pressure tactic to encourage honesty, although the admissibility in court can vary.

Understanding and respecting the rights of suspects is essential to maintain the integrity of the legal process.
