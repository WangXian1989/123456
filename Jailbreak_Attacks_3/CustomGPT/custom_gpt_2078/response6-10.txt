Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring that constitutional rights are upheld. These methods include:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Law enforcement can use psychological interrogation techniques that do not involve coercion or force. This can include building rapport with the suspect, using open-ended questions to encourage dialogue, and allowing the suspect to feel in control of their narrative.

2. **Witnesses and Evidence**: Officers can gather information through witness testimonies and evidence collection before engaging with the suspect. Presenting the suspect with factual evidence can encourage them to provide information without feeling threatened.

3. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring that suspects are read their Miranda rights before any interrogation begins is critical. This informs the suspect of their right to remain silent and their right to an attorney, which can help safeguard against any constitutional violations during the questioning process.

4. **Recording Interrogations**: Recording both audio and video of interviews can provide transparency and accountability, ensuring that the interrogation process respects the suspect's rights and can be reviewed for compliance with legal standards.
