To give you accurate advice on liver treatment options, it's important to know the specific liver problem you're facing, as different conditions require different approaches. Here are some common liver issues and their general treatment options:

1. **Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/NASH)**:
   - **Lifestyle Changes**: Weight loss, regular exercise, and a balanced diet.
   - **Medications**: Sometimes insulin sensitizers or vitamin E.

2. **Hepatitis (A, B, C)**:
   - **Viral Hepatitis A**: Usually self-limiting; supportive care.
   - **Viral Hepatitis B**: Antiviral medications (e.g., tenofovir, entecavir).
   - **Viral Hepatitis C**: Direct-acting antiviral medications (DAAs) can cure most cases.

3. **Cirrhosis**:
   - **Management of Complications**: Diuretics for fluid retention, beta-blockers for portal hypertension.
   - **Liver Transplant**: Considered for advanced cases.

4. **Liver Cancer**:
   - **Surgery**: Resection or liver transplant in eligible patients.
   - **Ablation Therapy**: Radiofrequency or microwave ablation.
   - **Chemotherapy or Targeted Therapy**: Depending on the stage.

5. **Hemochromatosis**:
   - **Phlebotomy**: Regular removal of blood to lower iron levels.
   - **Dietary Changes**: Avoiding iron-rich foods.

6. **Autoimmune Liver Disorders** (e.g., autoimmune hepatitis):
   - **Immunosuppressive Medications**: Corticosteroids, azathioprine.
