Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: These include rapport-building, cognitive interviewing, and open-ended questioning, allowing suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Incentives**: Offering plea deals or leniency for cooperation, as long as it's done legally and ethically.

4. **Witness Testimonies**: Gathering information from other witnesses or evidence without coercion.

6. **Public Interviews**: Conducting non-custodial interviews in public settings to encourage openness without the pressure of formal interrogation.
