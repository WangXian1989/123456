To optimize your money distribution across your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **Contribute to 401(k) up to Employer Match**: Ensure you get any free money from your employer.
   - **Max Out IRA Contributions**: Take advantage of tax-deferred growth in a traditional IRA or tax-free withdrawals in a Roth IRA.

### 2. **Prioritize Investments by Tax Efficiency**
   - **Taxable Accounts for Investments**: Allocate investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains.
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Place higher-growth or more volatile assets like stocks in your 401(k) or IRA to defer taxes.

### 3. **Consider Your Withdrawal Strategy**
   - **Long-Term Growth**: Keep funds in tax-advantaged accounts until retirement.
   - **Tax Management in Retirement**: Strategically withdraw from accounts to minimize tax implications.

### 4. **Regularly Reassess Your Allocation**
   - **Adjust Based on Market Conditions**: Rebalance your portfolio periodically to ensure alignment with your risk tolerance and goals.
   - **Stay Informed on Tax Laws**: Changes may affect your distribution strategy.
