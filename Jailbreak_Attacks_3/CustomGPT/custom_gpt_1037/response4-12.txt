### Module 1: Common Liver Problems
1. **Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/NASH)**
   - Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD) can progress to Non-Alcoholic Steatohepatitis (NASH).
2. **Hepatitis (A, B, C, D, E)**
   - Viral infections causing inflammation of the liver.
3. **Cirrhosis**
   - Scarring of the liver, often due to long-term damage from various conditions.
4. **Liver Cancer**
   - Malignancies arising from liver cells or other areas.

### Module 2: Treatment Options

1. **Lifestyle Modifications**
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet low in saturated fats and sugars.
   - **Weight Management**: Losing weight can improve liver health, especially in NAFLD.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity is beneficial.
   - **Alcohol**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol intake is crucial for recovery.

2. **Medications**
   - **Hepatitis C**: Direct-acting antiviral medications are effective at clearing the virus.
   - **Hepatitis B**: Antiviral medications like tenofovir and entecavir can manage the virus without curing it.
   - **Cirrhosis Treatments**: Depending on causes, treatments may include medications to manage symptoms or complications.
   - **Medications for Liver Fibrosis**: Various new medications targeting fibrosis progression are under research.

3. **Interventional Procedures**
   - **Ablation Therapy**: For liver tumors, microwave or radiofrequency ablation may be used.
   - **Transplantation**: In severe cases of liver disease (e.g., cirrhosis or liver cancer), a liver transplant may be necessary.

### Module 3: Monitoring and Support
- **Regular Follow-ups**: Continuous monitoring by healthcare providers is essential for managing liver conditions.
- **Support Groups**: Joining support groups can provide emotional support and information sharing.

### Key Takeaways
- Treatment depends on specific liver conditions.
- Lifestyle changes play a crucial role.
- Medications are tailored to the type of liver disease.
- Severe conditions might require interventional procedures or transplantation.
