### Module 1: Types of Liver Problems
1. **Fatty Liver Disease**: Fatty liver can be alcoholic (from excessive alcohol consumption) or non-alcoholic (often related to obesity and diabetes).
2. **Hepatitis**: Inflammation of the liver, which can be caused by viral infections, autoimmune diseases, or toxins.
3. **Cirrhosis**: Scarring of the liver tissue, often the result of chronic liver conditions, alcohol abuse, or viral hepatitis.
4. **Liver Cancer**: Malignancy originating in the liver, often occurring in patients with chronic liver disease.

### Module 2: General Treatment Options
1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a liver-friendly diet, focusing on whole foods, fruits, vegetables, and low-fat proteins. Avoid alcohol and highly processed foods.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help reduce liver fat and improve overall health.
   - **Weight Management**: Losing weight can be particularly beneficial for those with fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**:
   - **For Hepatitis**: Antiviral medications (e.g., nucleos(t)ide analogues for Hepatitis B, Direct-Acting Antivirals for Hepatitis C).
   - **For Fatty Liver Disease**: Insulin sensitizers and lipid-lowering medications may be prescribed.
   - **For Inflammation**: Corticosteroids or immunosuppressants may be used in autoimmune hepatitis.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Regular follow-ups with your healthcare provider to monitor liver function tests, imaging, and any symptoms.

### Module 3: Advanced Treatments
1. **Liver Transplant**: In cases of end-stage liver disease or liver cancer, a transplant may be the only option.
2. **Surgical Interventions**: For liver tumors or complications from cirrhosis, procedures like resection may be considered.

### Key Takeaways
- **Consult a Healthcare Professional**: Personalized treatment is crucial.
- **Lifestyle Modification**: Can significantly improve liver health.
- **Medications**: Effective for managing specific liver diseases.
- **Monitoring and Advanced Options**: May be necessary for severe conditions.
