Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights, including:

1. **Miranda Warnings**: Before questioning, officers must inform suspects of their right to remain silent and their right to an attorney.

2. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews in a non-coercive manner, ensuring the suspect feels free to leave or decline to answer questions.

3. **Good Cop/Bad Cop Technique**: Using a combination of friendly and confrontational approaches can lead to more open dialogue without coercion.

4. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship can encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

5. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique encourages suspects to recount events in detail, improving recall without pressure.

6. **Use of Non-Deceptive Techniques**: Officers can use open-ended questions, allowing suspects to provide information at their own pace.

7. **Psychological Strategies**: Techniques like emphasizing the benefits of cooperation or presenting facts can motivate suspects to divulge information.

9. **Legal Representation**: Allowing access to legal counsel during questioning can reinforce the protection of rights.
