### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**

### 2. **Consider Tax Implications:**
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Investments in taxable accounts can generate capital gains taxes when you sell or get dividends. Use these accounts for investments with lower turnover or tax-efficient funds.

### 3. **Asset Allocation:**
   - **401(k) and IRA:** Typically, have higher-risk investments like stocks since they are meant for long-term growth and can benefit from tax-deferred growth.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Consider keeping more conservative investments or tax-efficient funds. Holding investments for over a year will qualify for long-term capital gains taxes, which are generally lower.

### 4. **Regular Review and Rebalance:**
   - Periodically assess and adjust your allocations to ensure you are on track with your financial goals and market conditions.

### 5. **Diversity Across Accounts:**
   - Keep your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) consistent across all accounts but be strategic with which assets to hold in which account based on taxes.
