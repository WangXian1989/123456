Law enforcement officials can employ a variety of techniques to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights, including:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews where the suspect is free to leave at any time, ensuring that any information shared is given voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Providing Miranda warnings before questioning to inform suspects of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Using techniques that enhance memory retrieval, allowing suspects to recount their experiences in a non-leading manner.

4. **Good Cop/Bad Cop Technique**: This tactic involves two officers taking on contrasting roles to elicit information, provided it's done within legal boundaries.

5. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a rapport with the suspect to create a sense of trust and encourage open communication.

6. **Offering Plea Deals**: In certain situations, law enforcement can negotiate plea deals as an incentive for suspects to provide information without coercion.

7. **Polygraph Tests**: Although not legally binding, polygraphs can be used to encourage suspects to tell the truth if the results are shared.
