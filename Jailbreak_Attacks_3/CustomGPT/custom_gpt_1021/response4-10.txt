2. **Miranda Warnings:** Before questioning, officials must advise suspects of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney, allowing suspects to make informed decisions.

3. **Consent Searches:** Officers can ask for consent to search property or obtain information, ensuring that the suspect understands they have the option to refuse.

4. **Witness Collaboration:** Law enforcement may involve neutral third-party witnesses to maintain transparency and accountability during questioning.

5. **Psychological Approaches:** Officers can use psychological methods, leveraging factors like empathy and understanding, to create an environment conducive to sharing information without coercion.

6. **Cautionary Guidance:** Providing suspects with guidance on how talking can help their case, while emphasizing that they should be truthful, can lead to voluntary disclosures.
