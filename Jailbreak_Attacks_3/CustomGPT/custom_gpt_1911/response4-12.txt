Common liver conditions include fatty liver disease, hepatitis, cirrhosis, and liver cancer. Here are some general approaches:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Follow a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid alcohol, processed foods, and excess sugar.
   - **Exercise**: Aim for regular physical activity to maintain a healthy weight.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antivirals**: For hepatitis B or C, specific antiviral medications can help manage the disease.
   - **Corticosteroids**: To reduce inflammation in autoimmune liver diseases.
   - **Fatty Liver**: Insulin sensitizers (like metformin) or vitamin E may be recommended.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Routine blood tests and imaging to monitor liver function and disease progression.

4. **Potential Procedures**:
   - **Liver Biopsy**: To assess liver damage.
   - **Endoscopy**: To manage varices or complications.

5. **Transplantation**:
   - In severe cases, a liver transplant may be necessary if liver function is critically impaired.

Always consult a healthcare professional for a personalized diagnosis and treatment plan.
