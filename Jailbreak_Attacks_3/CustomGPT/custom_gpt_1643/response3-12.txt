### 1. **Diet and Lifestyle Changes**
   - **Balanced Diet**: Focus on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid processed foods, sugar, and saturated fats.
   - **Weight Management**: Maintain a healthy weight to reduce liver strain, especially if you have fatty liver disease.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help improve liver health and support weight loss.

### 2. **Medication**
   - **Antiviral Drugs**: For viral hepatitis, specific antiviral medications can help manage the infection.
   - **Corticosteroids**: These may be prescribed for autoimmune liver diseases.
   - **Liver Supplements**: Some may help, but consult your doctor first.

### 3. **Regular Monitoring**
   - **Blood Tests**: Keep track of liver enzyme levels, bilirubin, and liver function tests.
   - **Imaging Tests**: Ultrasounds or CT scans can monitor liver size and condition.

### 4. **Management of Underlying Conditions**
   - **Diabetes Management**: If diabetic, controlling blood sugar levels can reduce liver damage.
   - **Alcohol Abstinence**: Avoid alcohol completely if alcohol-related liver disease is present.

### 5. **Interventional Procedures**
   - **Endoscopy**: For variceal bleeding in advanced liver disease.
   - **Liver Transplantation**: In severe cases where liver function is critically impaired.

### 6. **Alternative Therapies**
   - **Herbal Remedies**: Some may offer benefits, but always discuss these with your doctor to avoid interactions.

Always consult with a healthcare professional for a thorough diagnosis and a treatment plan tailored to your specific situation.
