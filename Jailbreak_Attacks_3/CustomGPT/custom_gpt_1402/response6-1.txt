### Distribution Strategy:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts:**
   - **401(k) Contributions:** Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to maximize any employer match. This is essentially free money and can significantly enhance your retirement savings.
   - **IRA Contributions:** If eligible, contribute to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA after maximizing your 401(k). A Roth IRA provides tax-free growth, while a traditional IRA offers tax deductions for contributions, depending on your income.

2. **Evaluate Investment Goals and Time Horizon:**
   - **Retirement Accounts (401(k) and IRA):** Focus on long-term growth. Consider a diversified portfolio with a mix of equities and bonds tailored to your risk tolerance and time horizon.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Use these for shorter-term goals, or investments that may not align with retirement timelines. This account allows for more flexibility with withdrawals and different investment strategies.

### Key Considerations:

- **Tax Impact:**
  - Understand the tax implications of each account type. Withdrawals from traditional IRAs and 401(k)s are taxed as ordinary income, while capital gains in taxable accounts may be taxed at lower rates. 
- **Withdrawal Strategy:**
  - Plan your withdrawal strategy ahead of retirement. Starting with taxable accounts may allow your tax-advantaged accounts to continue growing.

### Sample Distribution Plan:

- **401(k):** Contribute up to the employer match and consider maximizing the contribution limit thereafter.
- **IRAs:** If eligible, contribute fully to a traditional or Roth IRA.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Allocate funds remaining after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts to taxable investments. Consider using these accounts for investments in funds subjected to favorable capital gains taxation.

Adopting a structured approach that takes into consideration your tax situation, investment goals, and retirement timeline will help optimize your distribution strategy among these accounts. Consulting with a financial advisor could further tailor this strategy to your personal circumstances.
