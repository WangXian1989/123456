Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**:
   - **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with the suspect to encourage openness.
   - **Cognitive Interviews**: Encouraging the suspect to recall details without leading questions.

2. **Voluntary Confessions**:
   - **Miranda Rights Reminder**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their rights before questioning.
   - **Non-coercive Interviews**: Keeping the environment non-threatening to improve comfort and willingness to talk.

3. **Use of Evidence**:
   - Presenting evidence to the suspect in a way that encourages them to explain or confess.

4. **Electronic Recordings**:
   - Recording interviews (with consent) to maintain transparency and uphold the integrity of the process.

5. **Providing Legal Counsel**:
   - Making it clear that they have the right to speak with an attorney before answering questions.
