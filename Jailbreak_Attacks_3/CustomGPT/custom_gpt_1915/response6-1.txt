When it comes to investing in your financial future, a well-thought-out strategy can make all the difference. Here are some tips for distributing your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match for your 401(k), aim to contribute at least enough to receive the full match. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings!

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Contributions to these accounts often reduce your taxable income. Traditional IRAs or 401(k)s lower your current tax bill, while Roth versions allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These are more flexible but can incur capital gains taxes. Use them for short-term goals or investments you want to access sooner.

3. **Diversify Investments**: Within these accounts, diversify your investments. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets to spread risk and improve potential returns.

4. **Conduct Regular Reviews**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on performance and your life changes, like retirement plans or major purchases.
