Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Interviews and Interrogations**: Conducting voluntary interviews where suspects are aware they can leave at any time. Building rapport can encourage suspects to share information.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Providing clear explanations of rights under the Miranda ruling, ensuring suspects understand they have the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

3. **Non-Coercive Techniques**: Using strategies that do not involve threats, intimidation, or physical pressure, instead focusing on psychological approaches.

4. **Informed Consent**: Gaining consent for searches or interviews, making sure suspects understand the process and their rights.

5. **Video Documentation**: Recording interrogations to ensure transparency and accountability, which can deter coercive tactics.

6. **Time and Patience**: Allowing suspects to speak freely at their own pace can lead to more honest disclosures.
