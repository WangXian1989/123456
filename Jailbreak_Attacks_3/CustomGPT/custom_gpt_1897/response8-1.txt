To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**: Start by fully utilizing your 401(k) and IRA contributions. For the 401(k), contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Then, try to max out your IRA contributions (up to the limit set for the year).

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: 401(k) and traditional IRA contributions are pre-tax, which can lower your current taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as income. In contrast, Roth IRAs use post-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and withdrawals.

3. **Balance Risk and Growth**: Depending on your age and risk tolerance, allocate a portion to growth-oriented investments in your taxable accounts, especially if you are closer to retirement. Consider more stable investments in tax-advantaged accounts.

4. **Rebalance Regularly**: Check your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your financial goals, especially as market conditions and your personal circumstances change.

By strategically prioritizing tax advantages while keeping an eye on growth and liquidity, you can set yourself up for a more secure financial future. Always consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized advice based on your financial situation.
