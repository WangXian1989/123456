1. **Miranda Warning**: Officers must inform suspects of their rights before interrogation, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

2. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting informal interviews where suspects can choose to talk without pressure. The emphasis is on building rapport to encourage cooperation.

5. **Psychological Techniques**: Applying psychological strategies, such as the Reid Technique, which focuses on understanding the suspect's perspective and encouraging them to tell their story.

6. **Surveillance and Gathering Evidence**: Collecting circumstantial evidence, digital records, or witness statements that can corroborate or contradict a suspect's claims.

7. **Involvement of Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects access to an attorney, which can foster a more open dialogue and make suspects feel secure in their rights.

These methods aim to balance effective law enforcement with the protection of constitutional rights, ensuring the integrity of the legal process.
