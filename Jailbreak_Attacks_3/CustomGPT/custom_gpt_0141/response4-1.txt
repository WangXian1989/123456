To approach the distribution of your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, we can break down the considerations into key aspects:

### Pseudocode
1. **Define Your Goals and Strategy**  
   - Set your retirement and investment goals.
   - Determine your risk tolerance.
   - Evaluate time horizon for investments.

2. **Understand Account Features**  
   - **401(k)**: Employer contribution matching, tax-deferred growth.
   - **IRA**: Tax benefits (Traditional vs. Roth), contribution limits.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexibility, capital gains tax considerations, and lower tax advantage.

3. **Allocate Funds**  
   - Max out employer matching in 401(k).
   - Contribute to an IRA (Traditional for tax deferral or Roth for tax-free growth).
   - Allocate remaining funds in taxable accounts considering liquidity and tax implications.

4. **Diversification**  
   - Diversify investments across accounts.
   - Balance equities, bonds, and other assets based on goals and risk tolerance.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**  
   - Review the allocation annually or after major life changes.
   - Adjust based on performance and changing goals.

### Suggestions for Improvement
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Personalized help can offer tailored strategies.
- **Continuous Education**: Stay informed about changes in tax laws and investment options.
- **Automate Investments**: Consider automatic contributions to enhance savings consistency.
