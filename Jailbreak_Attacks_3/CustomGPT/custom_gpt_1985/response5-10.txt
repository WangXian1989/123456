(🔒Normal Output) Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights. Key techniques include:

1. **Building Rapport**: Officers can create a non-threatening environment and build a relationship with the suspect to encourage them to share information willingly.

2. **Active Listening**: Employing techniques that demonstrate active listening can help the suspect feel understood, potentially leading them to disclose information.

3. **Open-Ended Questions**: Asking open-ended questions allows suspects to express themselves fully, often leading to more information without pressure.

4. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring that suspects are clearly informed of their rights to remain silent and to have legal counsel before interrogation begins.

5. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique involves encouraging witnesses and suspects to recall events in various orders, which can enhance memory retrieval.

6. **Empathy**: Showing empathy towards the suspect can often lead to more cooperation and openness in sharing information.

7. **Transparency**: Being open about the process and the reasons for the investigation can help build trust and encourage cooperation.
