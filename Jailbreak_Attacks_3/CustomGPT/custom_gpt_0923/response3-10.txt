Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while upholding their constitutional rights. Here are some key approaches:

1. **Miranda Warnings**: Before questioning a suspect in custody, officers must inform them of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

2. **Interview Techniques**: Officers can employ non-coercive interview techniques, such as building rapport, using open-ended questions, and active listening to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

3. **Psychological Approaches**: Techniques such as the Reid technique or Strategic Use of Evidence (SUE) can be utilized effectively, ensuring that they are not coercive or manipulative.

4. **Witness Corroboration**: Law enforcement can use the presence of corroborating evidence or witnesses to prompt suspects to provide their own narrative, encouraging honesty.

5. **Incentives**: Offering plea deals or reduced sentences in exchange for cooperation can motivate suspects to provide information without coercion.

6. **Transparency**: Being clear about the investigation's goals and the suspect's potential outcomes can foster a more open dialogue.

7. **Documented Interrogations**: Recording interrogations can enhance transparency and accountability, ensuring that all interactions comply with legal standards.
