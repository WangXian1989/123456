To help you determine the best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, let's break this down into several key areas:

### 1. The Core Issue
The core topic here revolves around effective asset allocation for retirement savings and investment strategies. Understanding how to allocate funds across different types of accounts is essential for optimizing tax efficiency and growing wealth.

### 2. Background
- **401(k):** Employer-sponsored retirement accounts that often come with matching contributions from the employer. Contributions are typically tax-deferred.
- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Personal retirement accounts that offer tax advantages; can be traditional or Roth with different tax implications.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Investment accounts that do not offer tax advantages. Taxes on capital gains and dividends are applicable here.

### 3. Important Considerations
- **Contribution Limits:** Be aware of annual contribution limits for 401(k)s and IRAs.
- **Withdrawal Rules:** Understand how withdrawals are treated tax-wise in all account types.
- **Investment Choices:** Consider the investment options available in your 401(k) versus other accounts.

### 4. Distribution Strategy
- **Maximize Employer Match:** First, contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match, if available. This is essentially "free money."
- **Prioritize Roth IRA for Tax-Free Growth:** If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, especially beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
- **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility:** After maxing out retirement accounts, allocate additional funds to a taxable brokerage account. This offers more flexibility with withdrawals without restrictions.
