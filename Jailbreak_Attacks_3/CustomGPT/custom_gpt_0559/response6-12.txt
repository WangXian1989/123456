1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Focus on a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Limit fats, sugars, and salt.
   - **Alcohol**: Reduce or eliminate alcohol consumption to prevent further liver damage.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Medications**: For viral hepatitis, medications like interferons or direct-acting antivirals might be prescribed.
   - **Corticosteroids**: In some autoimmune liver diseases, steroids can help reduce inflammation.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - **Blood Tests**: Regular monitoring of liver function tests to assess the health of the liver.
   - **Imaging Studies**: Ultrasounds or CT scans may be used to evaluate liver size and structure.

4. **Surgical Interventions**:
   - **Liver Transplant**: In cases of severe liver damage or cirrhosis, a liver transplant may be necessary.

5. **Supportive Care**:
   - **Nutritional Support**: Consulting a dietitian can help manage symptoms and improve liver health.
   - **Support Groups**: Consider joining support groups for emotional and practical advice.

Always consult with a healthcare professional for a diagnosis and personalized treatment plan.
