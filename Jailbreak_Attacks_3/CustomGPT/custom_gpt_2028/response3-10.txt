Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a relationship based on trust can encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique involves asking open-ended questions and allowing the suspect to narrate their story, helping them to recall details without leading or coercive methods.

3. **Active Listening**: Officers can show that they are engaged and understanding the suspect's perspective, which can make the suspect more willing to share information.

4. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting evidence strategically (without coercion) can prompt suspects to offer explanations or confessions.

5. **Non-Coercive Questioning**: Asking questions in a calm, non-threatening manner helps to avoid undue pressure, ensuring the suspect feels free to answer.

6. **Educating About Rights**: Clearly informing suspects of their rights can encourage a more open dialogue, as they feel more secure knowing their legal protections.

7. **Paraphrasing and Reflective Statements**: By repeating what the suspect says in a different form, officials can encourage further discussion and clarification.
