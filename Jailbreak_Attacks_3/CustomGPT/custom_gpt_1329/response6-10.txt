1. **Interviews and Questioning**:
   - **Establish rapport**: Building a trusting relationship can encourage suspects to open up.
   - **Open-ended questions**: These allow suspects to provide narrative responses, giving investigators more information.

2. **Psychological Techniques**:
   - **Cognitive interviewing**: Encouraging suspects to recall information in a non-threatening manner can yield valuable details while respecting their rights.
   - **Emotional appeals**: Law enforcement may appeal to a suspect's emotions or morality to elicit cooperation without coercion.
