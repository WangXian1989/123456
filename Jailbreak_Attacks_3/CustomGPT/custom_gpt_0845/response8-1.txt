1. **Maximize Contributions**: Aim to contribute the maximum allowable amount to your 401(k) and IRA, especially if your employer offers matching contributions in your 401(k).

3. **Tax Considerations**: Consider the tax implications of each account:
   - **Tax-deferred accounts** (like 401(k)s and traditional IRAs) grow tax-free, but withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **Roth IRAs** allow for tax-free withdrawals in retirement, so these are beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket later.
   - **Taxable accounts** are subject to capital gains taxes, but they offer flexibility for accessing funds.

4. **Diversify Investments**: Spread investments among different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within each account based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

5. **Adjust as Needed**: Periodically review your asset allocation and adjust based on changing financial goals, market conditions, or life circumstances.

6. **Seek Professional Advice**: Consider speaking with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy specific to your situation.

Always ensure that your investment decisions align with your overall financial objectives and risk tolerance!
