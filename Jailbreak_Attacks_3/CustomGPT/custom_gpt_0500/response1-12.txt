1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Dietary Adjustments**: Incorporating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and healthy fats while avoiding processed foods, alcohol, and high sugar.
   - **Regular Exercise**: Engaging in physical activity to help maintain a healthy weight and improve liver function.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Medications**: For viral hepatitis, specific antiviral medications can help manage the infection.
   - **Corticosteroids**: May be used for autoimmune liver diseases to reduce inflammation.
   - **Medication Management**: Adjusting any medications that may contribute to liver damage.

3. **Medical Procedures**:
   - **Liver Biopsy**: In some cases, to assess the extent of liver damage.
   - **Endoscopy**: For complications like variceal bleeding due to portal hypertension.

4. **Surgery**:
   - **Liver Transplantation**: For severe liver failure or when other treatments are ineffective.

5. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - **Routine Blood Tests**: Essential to monitor liver function and detect any changes early.
