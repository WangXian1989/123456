Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection can make suspects more comfortable and willing to share information.

2. **Interviews and Interrogations**: Using non-coercive questioning techniques, such as open-ended questions, can encourage suspects to provide information voluntarily.

3. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights ensures they are aware of their ability to remain silent and obtain legal counsel.

4. **Polygraph Tests**: While not always admissible in court, these tests can sometimes help elicit information when used appropriately.

5. **Incentives**: Offering plea deals or reduced charges can motivate suspects to provide information.
