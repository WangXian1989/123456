To optimally distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these factors:

1. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Max out employer match in your 401(k) first; it's essentially free money.
   - Then, consider contributing to an IRA (traditional or Roth) depending on your eligibility and tax situation.

2. **Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing taxable income. Withdrawals are taxed as regular income.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Contributions may be tax-deductible, and taxes are owed upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made after-tax, but withdrawals (if qualified) are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments may be taxed on dividends, interest, and capital gains, but provide flexibility and access.

3. **Investment Types**:
   - Hold more tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to minimize tax liabilities.
   - Keep tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts, which can help manage tax burdens due to capital gains.

   - Consider a mix of growth and income strategies based on your projected timeline.

5. **Personal Situation**:
   - Risk tolerance, retirement goals, and current financial situation greatly influence your allocation. Balance higher-risk investments for inflation protection vs. safer investments for stability.

A well-thought-out mix based on these factors can help you maximize your investment growth while minimizing taxes. Consulting with a financial advisor may also provide personalized strategies tailored to your specific situation.
