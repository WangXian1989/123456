Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Establishing rapport and using conversational approaches can help encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Informing suspects of their rights before questioning, ensuring they understand they can remain silent and have an attorney present.

3. **Non-Coercive Questioning**: Using open-ended questions and avoiding intimidation or threats to create a more comfortable environment.

4. **Building Trust**: Demonstrating empathy and understanding can make suspects more willing to provide information.

5. **Evidence Presentation**: Presenting evidence in a straightforward manner can lead suspects to confess or provide more details without coercion.

6. **Polygraph Tests**: While results are not always admissible in court, the process can sometimes lead to further questioning and information sharing.
