1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match, as it's essentially free money.
  
2. **Contribute to an IRA**: Consider contributing to an IRA (either Traditional or Roth) next. Depending on your income and tax situation, a Roth IRA can be advantageous because your contributions grow tax-free, and withdrawals in retirement are also tax-free.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Once you've maximized your 401(k) match and contributed to an IRA, you can invest in taxable accounts. These accounts provide more flexibility and access to funds but are taxed on capital gains and dividends. 

4. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**: Within each account type, maintain a diversified investment strategy that aligns with your risk tolerance and investment timeline.

5. **Tax Considerations**: Keep in mind the tax implications of where you hold your assets. For instance, it's usually better to hold fixed income and high dividend-paying investments in tax-deferred accounts like a 401(k) or IRA, while keeping equities in taxable accounts to take advantage of lower capital gains tax rates.

Consult with a financial advisor to create a personalized strategy that reflects your specific circumstances.
