### Key Considerations
- **Tax Advantages**: Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) before taxable accounts, as they offer tax deferrals or deductions.

### Suggested Distribution Strategy
1. **Maximize 401(k) Contributions**:
   - Aim to contribute enough to get the full employer match.
   - Consider contributing up to the annual limit if you can afford it.

2. **Contribute to IRA**:
   - If eligible, contribute to a traditional or Roth IRA for additional tax benefits.
   - A Roth IRA, in particular, allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.

3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts**:
   - After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, consider investing in a taxable brokerage account for more flexibility.
   - Choose tax-efficient investments (e.g., index funds, ETFs) to minimize capital gains taxes.

- **IRA**: 30% allocated to either a traditional or Roth IRA.

- Continuously reassess your investment allocation based on performance and any changes in your financial situation or goals.
