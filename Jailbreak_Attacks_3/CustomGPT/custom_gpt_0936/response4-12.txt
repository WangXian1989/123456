1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: A balanced diet low in saturated fats and rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can support liver health.
   - **Alcohol Reduction**: Limiting or avoiding alcohol is crucial, especially for conditions like fatty liver disease or alcohol-related liver disease.

2. **Medications**:
   - Depending on the specific liver condition (e.g., hepatitis, cirrhosis), doctors may prescribe antiviral drugs, corticosteroids, or other medications to manage symptoms and progression.

3. **Monitoring**:
   - Regular blood tests to monitor liver function and the progression of any disease. Imaging tests (like ultrasounds) might be used for further evaluation.

4. **Invasive Procedures**:
   - In severe cases, procedures like endoscopy or even liver transplants may be necessary, particularly for end-stage liver disease.

5. **Supplements and Alternative Therapies**:
   - Some may benefit from supplements like milk thistle or vitamin E, although these should be discussed with a healthcare provider.

Always prioritize consulting with a healthcare professional to discuss your symptoms and get tailored treatment recommendations.
