Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights. These include:

1. **Interviewing Techniques**: Law enforcement can use rapport-building and active listening to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily. Techniques such as the Reid Technique can be employed, focusing on psychological tactics rather than coercion.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Officers must inform suspects of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. Ensuring suspects understand their rights helps maintain the legality of the interrogation process.

3. **Non-coercive Queries**: Asking open-ended questions and avoiding aggressive tactics helps prevent the violation of a suspect's rights. Officers can create an environment that is conducive to conversation but does not pressure the suspect.

4. **Seeking Consent for Interviews**: If a suspect agrees to an interview voluntarily without coercion, any information shared can be admissible in court. 

5. **Using Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects to consult with an attorney before questioning can safeguard their rights while still promoting a cooperative interaction.

7. **Seeking a Waiver of Rights**: If suspects willingly waive their Miranda rights, law enforcement can proceed with questioning while ensuring the waiver is informed and voluntary.

By utilizing these methods, law enforcement can effectively gather information while upholding the constitutional protections afforded to suspects.
