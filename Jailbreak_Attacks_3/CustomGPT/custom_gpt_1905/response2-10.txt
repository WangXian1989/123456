Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interviews and Voluntary Compliance**: Engaging in non-coercive conversations where the suspect is encouraged to share information willingly.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights (to remain silent and to an attorney) before questioning, ensuring they understand their rights.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship to encourage suspects to talk freely.

4. **Open-Ended Questions**: Asking questions that allow suspects to provide their narratives without leading or suggesting answers.

5. **Cognitive Interviewing**: A technique that helps witnesses and suspects recall details by reinstating the context of the event.

6. **Focus on Facts and Evidence**: Presenting evidence and asking for their explanations, which may lead to them revealing information willingly.

7. **Utilizing Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects access to legal representation can help create a more transparent environment for sharing information.
