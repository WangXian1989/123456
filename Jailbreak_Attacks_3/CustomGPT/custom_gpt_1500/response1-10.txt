Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Officers can employ psychological tactics, such as rapport building and open-ended questions, to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Suspects must be informed of their rights to remain silent and to legal counsel before questioning, ensuring any information gathered is admissible.

3. **Voluntary Statements**: Encourage suspects to provide information willingly without coercion, emphasizing the benefits of cooperation.

4. **Witness and Expert Testimony**: Engaging with witnesses or experts can provide context or corroborative evidence that prompts suspects to speak up.

7. **Seeking Legal Counsel**: Offering suspects access to an attorney can help ensure that their rights are protected during the process.
