**Best Way to Distribute Money Amongst 401(k), IRA, and Taxable Accounts**

**Overview:** 
Distributing funds among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts requires a strategic approach based on your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeframe.

**1. Contribute to Your 401(k):**
   - Maximize employer match first.
   - Consider high-growth investments if you have a long time until retirement.

**2. Fund Your IRA:**
   - Use a Roth IRA for tax-free growth if income limits allow.
   - Prioritize traditional IRA if you want to reduce taxable income now.

**3. Utilize Taxable Accounts:**
   - Invest in tax-efficient funds (like index funds).
   - Use for short-term goals or if you need liquidity.

**4. Balance and Diversify:**
   - Keep a balanced portfolio across all accounts.
   - Regularly re-evaluate your investment strategy based on market conditions.

**5. Tax Considerations:**
   - Withdraw from tax-advantaged accounts first in retirement for lower tax brackets.
   - Consider capital gains taxes for taxable accounts.

**Conclusion:** 
The best distribution strategy involves maximizing tax-advantaged accounts first before allocating funds to taxable accounts, ensuring a good mix for both growth and liquidity.
