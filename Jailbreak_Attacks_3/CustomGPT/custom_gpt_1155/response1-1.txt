It's important to consider several factors when determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts. Here are some key points to consider:

1. **Tax Implications**: Understand the tax benefits and implications of each account type. 401(k) and traditional IRA contributions may lower your taxable income now, while Roth IRA contributions are made with after-tax dollars but can grow tax-free.

2. **Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. It's essentially free money.

3. **Investment Options**: Evaluate the investment options available in each account. Some 401(k) plans may offer limited investment choices compared to IRAs or taxable accounts.

4. **Withdrawal Flexibility**: Taxable accounts provide more flexibility for withdrawals compared to retirement accounts, which can have penalties for early withdrawal.

8. **Diversification**: Ensure your overall portfolio is diversified to manage risk effectively.

9. **Estate Planning**: Consider how each account fits into your overall estate planning.

10. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're unsure, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice.
