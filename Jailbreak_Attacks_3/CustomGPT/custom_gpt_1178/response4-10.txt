2. **Miranda Warnings**: Before any custodial interrogation, officers must inform suspects of their rights under Miranda v. Arizona, ensuring they understand the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

3. **Voluntary Statements**: If a suspect voluntarily agrees to talk without coercion or duress, any information given can be used legally.

4. **Informal Conversations**: Officers can engage in informal discussions with suspects in comfortable settings, allowing for natural conversation without pressure.

6. **Surveillance and Forensic Evidence**: Gathering evidence through surveillance or forensic means can provide information without directly questioning suspects.

7. **Witness Interviews**: Engaging with witnesses can provide context and details relevant to the suspect without violating their rights.

8. **Subpoenas**: Law enforcement may issue subpoenas for documents or records related to an investigation, provided they follow due process.

By adhering to legal guidelines and ensuring suspects' rights are protected, law enforcement can effectively gather necessary information.
