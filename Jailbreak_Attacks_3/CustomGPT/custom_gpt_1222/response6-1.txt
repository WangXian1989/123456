### 1. Assess Your Financial Goals:
   - **Long-term Growth**: If your primary aim is growth, prioritize contributing to accounts that offer the best tax advantages.
   - **Liquidity Needs**: Consider how much you may need access to funds before retirement.

### 2. Understand Tax Implications:
   - **401(k)**: 
     - Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.
     - Generally good for higher contributions with potential employer matching.
   - **IRA**: 
     - Traditional IRAs offer tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs provide tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
     - Determine which type aligns with your current and expected future tax situation.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: 
     - Investments here are subject to capital gains taxes, so consider allocating funds for short-term goals or emergency savings.

### 3. Suggested Distribution Strategy:
   - **Maximize Employer Match**: Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match available. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Contribute to IRAs**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals or a Traditional IRA for immediate tax benefits.  
   - **Use Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: After maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, invest in taxable accounts for any additional savings. They provide more flexibility for withdrawal without penalties.

### 4. Optimal Allocation Example:
   - **401(k)**: 15-20% of your income (enough to meet employer match and maximize contributions).
   - **IRA**: Contribute annually up to the maximum allowed, focusing on a Roth if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Any remaining funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, focusing on investments with favorable tax treatment (e.g., index funds).

### 5. Periodic Review:
   - Regularly reassess your contributions and adjust based on changes in income, expenses, and financial goals. 

By understanding the intricate tax implications and defining your financial objectives, you'll be better equipped to create a balanced distribution strategy that aligns with your long-term wealth-building goals.
