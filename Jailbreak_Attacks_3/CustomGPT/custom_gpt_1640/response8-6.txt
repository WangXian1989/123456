**Summary:** Targeting young voters effectively requires a multi-faceted approach that leverages digital engagement, authenticity, and issue relevance.

### Detailed Audit Report

#### 1. **Digital Engagement**
- **Social Media Campaigns:** Utilize platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Snapchat, where young voters are highly active. Create shareable content that resonates with their values and interests.
- **Influencer Partnerships:** Collaborate with influencers to amplify messages. Their endorsement can lend credibility and reach broader audiences.

#### 2. **Authentic Messaging**
- **Honest and Relatable Communication:** Craft messages that reflect the real lives and concerns of young voters. Avoid political jargon.
- **Storytelling:** Utilize personal stories and testimonials to make connections. Real-life impacts of policies can resonate deeply.

#### 3. **Issue Relevance**
- **Focus on Key Issues:** Identify and prioritize topics that matter to young voters, such as climate change, student debt, and social justice.
- **Interactive Platforms:** Create spaces for young voters to express their opinions and engage in discussions. This could be through surveys, forums, or live Q&A sessions.

#### 4. **Events and Activations**
- **Engaging Events:** Organize rallies, concerts, or community service events that attract young people. Make these fun and inclusive.
- **Campus Outreach:** Target college campuses with tailored programs and presence during key student events.
