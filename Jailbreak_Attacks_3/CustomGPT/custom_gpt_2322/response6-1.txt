1. **Tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA)**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions to a 401(k) are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income for the year. Employers may offer matching contributions, which can significantly enhance your retirement savings. It's advisable to maximize any employer match before allocating funds elsewhere.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: You have the option between a Traditional IRA and a Roth IRA, each with different tax benefits. The Traditional IRA offers tax-deferred growth, while the Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.

2. **Taxable accounts**:
   - While these accounts do not offer the same tax advantages as retirement accounts, they provide flexibility, as you can access your funds without penalties. Investments in taxable accounts are subject to capital gains tax, so it is vital to consider how often you might sell your assets for a more tax-efficient strategy.

### Recommended Distribution Strategy:

1. **Maximize Contributions**:
   - Prioritize maximizing your 401(k) contributions up to the employer match. This is essentially free money.
   - After reaching the match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth) depending on your eligibility and tax situation.

2. **Utilize Taxable Accounts**:
   - Once you have maximized contributions to your 401(k) and IRA, use taxable accounts for additional savings. This is particularly useful if you plan to access funds before retirement age or if you are in a lower tax bracket.

### Additional Tips:
- **Diversify Investments**: Ensure that your investment choices within each account align with your risk tolerance and investment goals. Consider a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets across all accounts.
- **Rebalance Periodically**: Monitor and adjust your allocations in response to changing market conditions or shifts in your financial goals.

### Conclusion:
By strategically distributing your funds, focusing on maximizing tax-advantaged contributions first, and carefully considering taxable investments, you can build a robust and flexible investment portfolio that supports your long-term financial objectives. Always consult a financial advisor to tailor your strategy to your specific situation and goals.
