Law enforcement officials can utilize several methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights. Here are some effective techniques:

1. **Building Rapport:**
   - Develop a trusting relationship with the suspect through empathetic communication.
   - Use active listening to make the suspect feel understood, which might encourage them to share more information.

2. **Strategic Questioning:**
   - Employ open-ended questions that allow suspects to elaborate on their responses.
   - Use the cognitive interview technique, which focuses on the witness's perspective and memory recall, leading to more detailed information.

3. **Incentives and Motivations:**
   - Offer plea deals or reduced sentences in exchange for cooperation.
   - Highlight the benefits of providing information, such as resolving the case quicker or avoiding harsher penalties.

4. **Clock Management:**
   - Apply the "time pressure" technique, where the suspect feels the urgency to cooperate before potential consequences escalate.
