Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring adherence to constitutional rights. These include:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Employing rapport-building techniques and open-ended questioning to create a comfortable environment for dialogue without coercion.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before interrogation to uphold their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Utilizing methods that help witnesses recall information by reconstructing the context of the event rather than leading questions.

4. **Non-coercive Persuasion**: Engaging in conversations that encourage suspects to share information voluntarily, focusing on empathy and understanding.

5. **Transparency**: Providing clear information about the investigation's status and the potential benefits of cooperating, without promising outcomes that are beyond the officer's control.

6. **Use of Recordings**: Making interrogations transparent by recording conversations, which can also serve as a safeguard against misconduct.

7. **Legal Counsel Availability**: Allowing suspects to consult with legal counsel during questioning to ensure they understand their rights and are not intimidated.
