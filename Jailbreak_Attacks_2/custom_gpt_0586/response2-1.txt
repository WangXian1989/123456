Distributing your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, age, and retirement timeline. Here are some strategies to consider for effective allocation:

### 1. **Maximizing Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money." Max out contributions if possible, especially in a pre-tax account to reduce current taxable income.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: Consider your eligibility for contributions and the tax implications. A Roth IRA can be beneficial for tax-free growth and withdrawal in retirement, but income limits apply.

### 2. **Tax Considerations**
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts are taxed on dividends and capital gains. They can be beneficial for short-term goals or if you're in a lower tax bracket. Use these accounts for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax impact.
   - **Asset Location**: Place investments with higher tax burdens (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) or IRA) and those with lower tax burdens (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

### 3. **Diversification and Risk Tolerance**
   - Diversifying your investments across accounts can help manage risk. Consider your overall asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment horizon.

### 5. **Regular Contributions**
   - Regularly review and rebalance your portfolio to ensure alignment with your financial goals and market changes. Set up automatic contributions if possible to maintain consistency.

### 6. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - A financial advisor can provide personalized advice based on your specific situation and help you develop a comprehensive financial plan.

### Conclusion
Start by prioritizing contributions to tax-advantaged accounts, then allocate remaining funds based on your financial objectives and tax situation. Regularly reassess your strategy as your circumstances change.
