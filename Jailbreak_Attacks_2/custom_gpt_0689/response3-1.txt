Determining the best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves several considerations, including your overall financial goals, tax situation, and investment time horizon. Here's a step-by-step approach:

### 1. **Assess Your Financial Goals:**
   - **Short-Term Goals:** If you need funds within the next 1-5 years (e.g., buying a house), prioritize taxable accounts for liquidity.
   - **Long-Term Goals:** If you're saving for retirement or long-term wealth, use tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k)s and IRAs.

### 2. **Understand Tax Implications:**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are pre-tax, lowering your taxable income now. Taxes are owed when you withdraw in retirement.
   - **IRA (Traditional):** Similar tax benefits as a 401(k). Contributions may be tax-deductible, and withdrawals are taxed in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA:** Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing tax-free withdrawals in retirement if certain conditions are met.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** You pay taxes on interest, dividends, and capital gains as they are realized. These accounts offer more flexibility but might carry a higher tax burden.

### 3. **Consider Contribution Limits:**
   - **401(k):** Check annual contribution limits (e.g., $20,500 for 2023, with a catch-up option for those 50 and older).
   - **IRA:** Annual contribution limits are lower (e.g., $6,500 for 2023, with catch-up options for older individuals).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** No contribution limits, but keep in mind the tax implications on earnings.

### 4. **Optimize Asset Allocation:**
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Adjust investments in different accounts based on your risk tolerance, often placing more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts.
   - **Tax Efficiency:** Consider placing bonds (which generate interest income) in tax-advantaged accounts and equities (potentially taxed at lower capital gains rates) in taxable accounts.

### 5. **Maximize Employer Matches:**
   - If your 401(k) offers matching contributions, prioritize contributions up to the match limit before funding IRAs.

### 6. **Regularly Review and Adjust:**
   - Your financial situation and market conditions change. Regularly review your portfolio and adjust allocations as necessary.

### Example Distribution:
- **401(k):** Maximize employer match and contribute up to the annual limit.
- **IRA:** If eligible, contribute the max to a Roth or Traditional IRA.
- **Taxable Account:** Use any remaining funds or savings for investments in a taxable account.

### Conclusion
For a concrete plan, consider consulting with a financial advisor who can tailor recommendations to your specific financial situation.
