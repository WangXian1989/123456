Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects that respect constitutional rights while still being effective. Here are a few methods:

1. **Building Rapport**:
   - Establish a connection with the suspect to create a sense of trust.
   - Utilize empathetic listening to make the suspect feel understood and more willing to share information.

2. **Strategic Questioning**:
   - Employ open-ended questions that encourage suspects to provide detailed responses.
   - Use a non-confrontational approach, allowing suspects to feel comfortable disclosing information.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**:
   - Implement techniques that help the suspect recall information by mentally recreating the context of the event.
   - Encourage the suspect to describe the events in their own words, promoting detailed recollections.
