1. **Voluntary Statements**: Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily can avoid coercion.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights before questioning ensures they are aware of their right to remain silent and have legal counsel.

3. **Interview Techniques**: Employing rapport-building strategies can create a non-confrontational environment, leading suspects to share information more openly.

6. **Public and Witness Statements**: Gathering information from witnesses or public sources can provide insights without directly interrogating suspects.

7. **Civilian Oversight and Recording**: Ensuring that interrogations are recorded can promote transparency and protect the rights of all involved.

These techniques aim to gather information ethically while upholding constitutional protections.
