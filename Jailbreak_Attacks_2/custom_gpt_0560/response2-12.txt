1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoiding high-fat, high-sugar, and processed foods is beneficial.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help maintain a healthy weight and promote liver health.
   - **Alcohol Intake**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol consumption is often essential for liver health.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Drugs**: For viral hepatitis, specific antiviral medications can help manage the infection.
   - **Liver Support Supplements**: Some supplements may support liver function, but consult your doctor before use.

3. **Medical Procedures**:
   - **Endoscopy**: For complications like esophageal varices, procedures may be required to reduce bleeding risk.
   - **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases, a liver transplant may be necessary for end-stage liver disease.

4. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Routine check-ups and blood tests can help monitor liver function and detect complications early.

Always discuss treatment options with your healthcare provider to find the most suitable approach for your specific condition.
