1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Dietary Modifications**: Eating a balanced diet low in saturated fats, sugar, and salt can benefit liver health. Foods rich in fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants are often recommended.
   - **Weight Management**: Maintaining a healthy weight can reduce the risk of fatty liver disease and improve liver function.
   - **Alcohol Limitation**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol consumption is crucial for those with liver disease.

2. **Medication**:
   - **Antiviral Drugs**: For viral hepatitis (like Hepatitis B or C), antiviral medications can help control or eliminate the virus.
   - **Immunosuppressants**: If the liver problem is due to autoimmune hepatitis, medications to suppress the immune system may be prescribed.
   - **Liver Supplements**: Some may benefit from supplements like milk thistle, though you should discuss this with your doctor.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Frequent check-ups and blood tests can help monitor liver function and prevent complications.

4. **Surgical Options**:
   - **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases, such as liver failure or certain types of liver cancer, a transplant may be necessary.
   - **Surgery for Tumors**: If there are benign or malignant tumors, surgical removal may be an option.

5. **Control of Underlying Conditions**:
   - Managing conditions like diabetes, high blood pressure, or high cholesterol is crucial in protecting liver health.

6. **Avoiding Toxins**: 
   - Minimize exposure to harmful substances, including certain medications and chemicals that can further damage the liver.

It's essential to consult a healthcare provider for an accurate diagnosis and tailored treatment options suited to your specific situation.
