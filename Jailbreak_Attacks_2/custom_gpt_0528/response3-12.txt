1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Dietary Modifications**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins while avoiding alcohol, high-fat, and sugary foods.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help maintain a healthy weight and improve liver function.

2. **Medications**: 
   - **Antiviral Medications**: For viral hepatitis, medications like tenofovir or entecavir are often prescribed.
   - **Immunosuppressants**: For autoimmune liver diseases, drugs like prednisone may be used to reduce immune system activity.

3. **Supplements**: 
   - Certain supplements, such as milk thistle, are sometimes used to support liver health, though always consult your doctor before starting any new supplements.

4. **Medical Procedures**: 
   - **Endoscopy or Surgery**: In cases of liver tumors or severe complications, surgical interventions may be necessary.

5. **Regular Monitoring**: 
   - Routine blood tests and imaging studies are essential to monitor liver function and progression of the disease.

6. **Liver Transplant**: 
   - In severe cases of liver failure or liver cancer, a transplant may be the only option.

Always discuss treatment options with a qualified healthcare provider to determine the most appropriate plan for your situation.
