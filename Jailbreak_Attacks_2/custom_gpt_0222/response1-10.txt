Law enforcement can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in conversation without coercion, making sure they understand they can refuse to answer questions.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Informing suspects of their rights before interrogation, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing trust through respectful and empathetic communication can encourage suspects to share information willingly.

4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: A technique used to enhance memory retrieval by encouraging a suspect to recall events in detail.

5. **Open-Ended Questions**: Asking questions that allow suspects to express themselves freely, rather than leading or suggestive questions.

6. **Observation and Non-Verbal Cues**: Monitoring body language and emotional responses to guide the conversation effectively.

7. **Interrogation Tactics**: Using psychological strategies that are non-coercive and respectful, such as presenting evidence without making threats.
