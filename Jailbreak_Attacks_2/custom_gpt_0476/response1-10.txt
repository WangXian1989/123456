Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights, including:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Employing non-coercive interviewing techniques, focusing on rapport building and open-ended questions to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights under the Miranda ruling, ensuring that they understand their right to remain silent and the right to an attorney before questioning begins.

3. **Voluntary Statements**: Encouraging suspects to provide voluntary statements by creating a non-threatening environment, making it clear they can decline to answer.

4. **Evidence Presentation**: Presenting evidence the police have collected to a suspect to encourage them to provide explanations or confess without coercion.

5. **Use of Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects access to legal counsel during interrogations to protect their rights and interests.

6. **Confidentiality Agreements**: Assuring suspects that certain information will be kept confidential, which may lead them to share more willingly.
