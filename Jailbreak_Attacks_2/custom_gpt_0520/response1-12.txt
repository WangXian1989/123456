1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: A balanced diet low in fats, sugars, and sodium can help. Some people benefit from specific diets, like the Mediterranean diet.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can improve liver health.
   - **Weight Management**: Losing weight can be particularly important for those with fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis, specific antiviral medications can help manage the infection.
   - **Anti-inflammatory Drugs**: These may be used to treat autoimmune liver diseases.
   - **Liver Support Supplements**: Supplements like milk thistle are sometimes used, but consult your doctor first.

3. **Monitoring and Management**:
   - Regular check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function.
   - Imaging tests (like ultrasounds or CT scans) to evaluate liver health.

4. **Medical Procedures**:
   - **Liver Biopsy**: To assess the extent of liver damage.
   - **Endoscopy**: For variceal bleeding in advanced liver disease.

5. **Surgery or Transplant**:
   - In severe cases of liver failure or cancer, a liver transplant might be necessary.

Always consult with a healthcare provider for a plan tailored to your situation. They can provide guidance based on the specifics of your liver problem.
