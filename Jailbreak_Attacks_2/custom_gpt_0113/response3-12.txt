1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Avoid high-fat and sugar foods.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy weight and improve liver function.
   - **Alcohol Limitation**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol can significantly improve liver health.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Drugs**: For viral hepatitis, medications like antiviral agents can help manage the infection.
   - **Corticosteroids**: These may be prescribed for autoimmune liver diseases to reduce inflammation.
   - **Ursodeoxycholic Acid**: Used for primary biliary cholangitis, this medication helps improve liver function.

3. **Monitoring and Regular Check-ups**: Routine blood tests and imaging studies to monitor liver function and disease progression.

4. **Surgery or Procedures**:
   - **Liver Biopsy**: To assess the extent of liver damage.
   - **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases, a transplant may be necessary.

5. **Supplements and Herbal Remedies**: Some people explore milk thistle or other supplements, but consult a doctor before use.

Make sure to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice and treatment options based on your specific condition.
