It's important to consider various factors when allocating funds among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts. Here are some general guidelines to help you determine the best strategy for your situation:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to take full advantage of it. This is essentially free money.

2. **Contribute to a Roth IRA if Eligible**: If your income allows, consider contributing to a Roth IRA. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but qualified withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

3. **Tax Considerations**: 
   - **401(k)** and **Traditional IRA** contributions may reduce your taxable income in the year you contribute, but you will owe taxes when you withdraw money in retirement.

4. **Investment Strategy**: Consider placing investments that are likely to generate high short-term gains (which would be taxed heavily in a taxable account) into tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs) to defer or eliminate taxes.

5. **Diversification**: Ensure that your investments across all accounts are diversified to minimize risk.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan how you will withdraw funds in retirement, as this will affect how much you should keep in each account type.

7. **Contribution Limits**: Be mindful of annual contribution limits for each account type to optimize tax benefits.

8. **Financial Goals and Timeline**: Consider your financial goals, risk tolerance, and investment time horizon when allocating funds.

Ultimately, it may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your individual circumstances.
