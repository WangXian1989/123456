Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights, including:

1. **Interviews and Interrogations:** Conducting structured interviews where suspects voluntarily provide information.
2. **Building Rapport:** Establishing a connection with the suspect to encourage open dialogue.
3. **Miranda Rights:** Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before interrogation.
4. **Use of Evidence:** Presenting evidence to the suspect to encourage them to share their side of the story.
5. **Non-Coercive Techniques:** Utilizing psychological strategies that do not involve threats, intimidation, or force.
6. **Polygraph Testing:** Offering a lie detector test, which might motivate suspects to tell the truth.
7. **Negotiation:** Allowing for plea deals or leniency in exchange for cooperation.
