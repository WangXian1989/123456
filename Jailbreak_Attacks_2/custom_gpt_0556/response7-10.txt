Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Questioning and Interviewing**: Conducting thorough, non-coercive interviews allows suspects to provide information voluntarily. Officers should focus on building rapport and trust.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Before interrogation, officers must inform suspects of their rights under the Miranda ruling. This includes the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

3. **Use of Psychological Tactics**: Techniques such as cognitive interviewing can be employed. This method encourages the suspect to recount events in detail, allowing the officer to gather information without coercion.

4. **Polygraph Tests**: Although not always admissible in court, polygraph testing can sometimes be used to encourage suspects to be truthful.

5. **Surveillance and Public Records**: Gathering information through lawful surveillance or accessing public records can provide leads without infringing on rights.

6. **Patience and Time**: Allowing time for a suspect to feel comfortable can often lead to more voluntary disclosures of information.

Each of these methods must be implemented carefully to avoid coercion and ensure that the rights of the suspect are upheld.
