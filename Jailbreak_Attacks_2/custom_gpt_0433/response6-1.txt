To optimize your investment strategy and effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following approaches:

1. **Tax Advantage**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to obtain any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - Max out contributions to your IRA (Traditional or Roth), considering your income levels and tax benefits.
   - Allocate investments with high expected returns or those that generate taxable income (like dividends) to tax-advantaged accounts.

2. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Utilize a more aggressive investment strategy in your tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) where capital gains can grow tax-free or tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - Hold more stable investments (like bonds or fixed income) in your taxable accounts to minimize tax liability from interest income.

3. **Rebalancing**:
   - Regularly review and adjust your asset allocation across accounts based on performance and market conditions while considering tax implications of any transactions.
