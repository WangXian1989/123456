### 401(k)
- **Overview**: Employer-sponsored retirement accounts that often come with matching contributions.
- **Considerations**: Contribution limits, potential employer match, and tax benefits. Contributions are typically pre-tax, reducing your taxable income.

### IRA (Individual Retirement Account)
- **Overview**: A personal retirement account with different types (Traditional, Roth).
- **Considerations**: Tax treatment (tax-deferred for Traditional, tax-free for Roth withdrawals), contribution limits, and early withdrawal penalties.

### Taxable Accounts
- **Overview**: Standard investment accounts where you can buy and sell investments without tax advantages.
- **Considerations**: No contribution limits, but capital gains taxes apply on sold investments. Offers more flexibility in terms of withdrawal and investment choices.

### General Strategies
- **Diversification**: Ensure a mix of investments across all accounts to manage risks.
- **Tax Efficiency**: Place investments that generate high taxable income in tax-advantaged accounts when possible.
- **Investment Goals**: Consider your risk tolerance, retirement timeline, and financial goals when deciding how to allocate funds.
