1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: Eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats can help support liver health.
   - **Alcohol Intake**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol can significantly improve liver function, especially if alcohol is a contributing factor to the liver problem.
   - **Weight Management**: Achieving a healthy weight through diet and exercise can help reduce the risk of fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**: 
   - Depending on the liver condition (like hepatitis), antiviral medications or corticosteroids might be prescribed.
   - For conditions like fatty liver disease, some medications that help regulate blood sugar or cholesterol levels may be beneficial.

3. **Regular Monitoring**: 
   - Regular check-ups and liver function tests may be necessary to monitor the condition and adjust treatment as needed.

4. **Avoiding Toxins**: 
   - Reducing exposure to toxins, including certain medications that can harm the liver, is essential.

5. **Surgery**: 
   - In severe cases, such as liver cirrhosis or tumors, liver transplant or other surgical interventions might be considered.
