Now, regarding your investment distribution among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to consider a few factors:

1. **Tax Implications**: 401(k) and IRA contributions often reduce your taxable income in the short-term, while taxable accounts may incur capital gains taxes when you sell investments.

2. **Retirement Goals**: Think about how soon you'll need access to these funds. If you're investing for the long term (retirement), prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts like a 401(k) or IRA can be beneficial.

3. **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual contribution limits for each type of account. For instance, 401(k) limits tend to be higher than IRA limits.

4. **Investment Options**: Consider the investment choices available in your 401(k) versus what you can select in your IRA and taxable accounts.

5. **Diversification**: Try to maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts to mitigate risk.

A general strategy could be to max out your 401(k) employer match (if available) first, then contribute to an IRA, and finally invest any additional funds into a taxable account. Adjust this strategy based on your personal financial situation and goals. Consulting with a financial advisor can also provide tailored advice.
