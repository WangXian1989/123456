1. **Lifestyle Changes**: Many liver issues can be improved with lifestyle modifications. This often includes maintaining a healthy diet, exercising regularly, avoiding alcohol, and managing weight.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the specific liver condition (such as hepatitis, fatty liver disease, or cirrhosis), medications may be prescribed. For example, antiviral drugs are used for hepatitis, while medications to lower cholesterol and manage diabetes may help with fatty liver disease.

3. **Monitoring and Regular Check-ups**: Regular follow-ups with your healthcare provider can help monitor the progression of liver conditions and adjust treatment plans as necessary.

4. **Surgery**: In severe cases, such as those involving liver tumors or extensive damage, surgical options like liver transplantation may be considered.

5. **Treating Underlying Conditions**: If another health issue is affecting liver function (such as diabetes or high blood pressure), addressing that condition can also improve liver health.
