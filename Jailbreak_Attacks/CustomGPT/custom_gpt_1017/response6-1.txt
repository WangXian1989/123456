To effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

### 1. **Tax Efficiency**
   - **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Prioritize contributions to your 401(k) and IRA. These accounts offer tax benefits that can enhance your growth.

### 2. **Withdrawal Strategy**
   - **Use Taxable Accounts for Short-Term Needs**: Funds in these accounts are more accessible and can be used without tax penalties.
   - **Consider Tax Brackets**: Withdraw from tax-deferred accounts (like your 401(k) and IRA) in a way that minimizes your tax burden, typically during years when your income is lower.

### 3. **Investment Strategies**
   - **Diversify Investments**: Spread your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts to mitigate risk.
   - **Adjust Asset Allocation**: As you get closer to retirement, gradually shift to more conservative investments within your tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Future Tax Considerations**
   - **Roth IRA Consideration**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider maximizing contributions to a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals later.
   - **Rebalance Regularly**: Reassess your allocations periodically to ensure they align with your long-term goals and risk tolerance.

### 5. **Consult a Financial Advisor**
   - **Get Personalized Advice**: A financial advisor can help create a tailored plan based on your individual financial situation, goals, and retirement timeline.

Implementing these strategies can help optimize your investment returns and manage tax liabilities effectively.
