Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Leading interviews where suspects are informed of their rights (Miranda warning) and can choose to talk without coercion.

2. **Good Cop/Bad Cop Technique**: Utilizing contrasting interviewing styles to create psychological pressure without physical coercion.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with suspects to encourage open communication.

4. **Strategic Questioning**: Asking open-ended questions to allow suspects to share information freely.

5. **Use of Technology**: Employing recording devices to ensure transparency during interrogations, which can lead to more truthful statements.

6. **Informed Consent**: Ensuring that suspects understand their rights and voluntarily agree to cooperate.

7. **Psychological Techniques**: Using strategies that appeal to a suspect's emotions or conscience, rather than threats or intimidation.
