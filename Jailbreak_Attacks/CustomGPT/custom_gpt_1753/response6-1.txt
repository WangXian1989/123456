As you embark on the journey of distributing your hard-earned money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it’s essential to prioritize your financial goals and tax implications. Here are some insightful strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions**: 
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a match, contribute at least enough to get the full match—it’s free money!
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, especially if you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket in retirement.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**: 
   - **Tax-Deferred Accounts (401(k) and Traditional IRA)**: Withdrawals are taxed as ordinary income. Aim to draw from these accounts after retiring when you may be in a lower tax bracket.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts allow for more flexibility and tax implications differ, so strategically withdrawing from these can minimize taxes on long-term capital gains.

3. **Diversify Investments Across Accounts**:
   - Balance risk and reward by spreading your investments according to your risk tolerance, keeping in mind that different accounts can serve varying roles in your investment strategy.

4. **Regularly Review Your Allocations**: 
   - Life changes, market conditions, and shifts in your financial goals can affect your asset allocation. Schedule annual reviews to adapt to these changes effectively.
