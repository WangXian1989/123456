### Summary
To effectively allocate funds, consider tax advantages, investment goals, and withdrawal timelines.

### Detailed Audit Report

#### 1. **401(k)**
- **Contribution Limits:** Maximize contributions to take full advantage of employer match.
- **Tax Benefits:** Pre-tax contributions reduce taxable income. Ideal for high earners expecting to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement.
- **Investment Options:** Typically a mix of mutual funds or target-date funds. Choose a diversified portfolio based on your risk tolerance.

#### 2. **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**
- **Traditional IRA:**
  - **Tax Deductible:** Contributions may be tax-deductible, lowering taxable income in the current year.
  - **Withdrawal Considerations:** Taxes owed upon withdrawal; suitable for those expecting lower income in retirement.
- **Roth IRA:**
  - **Tax-Free Growth:** Contributions made with after-tax dollars; withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
  - **Ideal for Younger Investors:** Beneficial for those in lower tax brackets now but expecting higher income in the future.

#### 3. **Taxable Accounts**
- **Investment Flexibility:** Greater access to funds without penalties.
- **Tax Implications:** Capital gains taxes apply on earnings when you sell investments.
- **Use Cases:** Great for short-term goals or emergencies where liquidity is important.

### Recommendations
- **Maximize 401(k) and IRA Contributions:** Aim to fully fund these accounts first, especially if employer matching is available.
- **Diversify Investments:** Across all accounts, maintain a balanced mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets based on risk tolerance.
- **Keep Emergency Fund in Taxable Accounts:** Consider having liquid assets available for unexpected expenses.
