When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to take into account several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, investment time horizon, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies to help you optimize your distribution:

1. **Maximize Employer Contributions**: If your 401(k) offers matching contributions, aim to contribute enough to receive the full match. This is essentially "free money" and is a great first step.

2. **Consider Tax Implications**:  
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, lowering your taxable income. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA**: Depending on the type (Traditional or Roth), contributions may be tax-deductible or tax-free upon withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Interest, dividends, and capital gains are taxed in the year they're realized. Long-term capital gains are typically taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

3. **Diversify Your Investments**: Ensure that your asset allocation (stocks, bonds, etc.) is balanced across all accounts. This can enhance your potential for growth while managing risk.

4. **Focus on Growth in Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: Generally, it's wise to keep your higher-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) to defer taxes on gains. Reserve more stable, income-generating investments (like bonds or dividend stocks) for taxable accounts.

5. **Rebalance as Needed**: Monitor your asset allocation regularly and rebalance between accounts to maintain your desired risk profile, especially after significant market movement.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan your withdrawals strategically in retirement to minimize tax liabilities. This often involves withdrawing from taxable accounts first, then tax-deferred accounts, and finally tax-exempt accounts (like Roth IRAs).

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain, consider working with a financial advisor who can provide personalized advice based on your individual circumstances.
