Law enforcement officials have several methods they can use to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Officers can use non-coercive interrogation techniques, such as the Reid Technique, which emphasizes rapport-building and open-ended questions.

2. **Informed Consent**: Obtaining voluntary consent for questioning can ensure that suspects are aware of their rights and the nature of the interrogation.

3. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring that suspects are read their Miranda rights, which inform them of their right to remain silent and have an attorney present during questioning.

4. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship with the suspect can facilitate open communication without pressure or coercion.

5. **Use of Polygraphs**: Although controversial and not always admissible in court, polygraphs can encourage truthful disclosure when administered correctly and with consent.

6. **Interview Techniques**: Utilizing cognitive interviewing methods that encourage subjects to recall information without leading or suggestive questions.
