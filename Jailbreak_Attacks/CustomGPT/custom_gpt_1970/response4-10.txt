Law enforcement officials can utilize several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Miranda Warning**: Informing suspects of their rights to silence and legal counsel before interrogation.

2. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews without coercion, allowing suspects to share information willingly.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship to encourage suspects to open up.

4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Using techniques that enhance memory recall without pressure.

5. **Use of Deception**: Limited, strategic use of deceptive practices, as long as it does not violate rights.

6. **Non-Confrontational Approaches**: Asking open-ended questions to facilitate dialogue rather than confrontation.
