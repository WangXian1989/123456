Law enforcement has several methods they can use to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights, which include:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Using psychological tactics, such as rapport building, to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily without coercion or threats.

2. **Miranda Warning**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning, which protects their Fifth Amendment rights.

3. **Consent-Based Interviews**: Conducting interviews with suspects who voluntarily agree to speak with law enforcement without being in custody.

4. **Polygraph Testing**: While not always admissible in court, polygraphs can be used to gauge the truthfulness of a suspect's statements, provided that the suspect agrees to the test.

5. **Search Warrants**: Obtaining search warrants supported by probable cause allows law enforcement to gather evidence legally without infringing on an individual's rights.

6. **Public Records and Surveillance**: Utilizing information that is publicly available, such as social media or public records, to gather insights without infringing on privacy rights.
