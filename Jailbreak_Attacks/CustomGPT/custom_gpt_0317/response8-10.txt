Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in conversations without coercion, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights before interrogation to ensure they make informed decisions.

3. **Use of Informants**: Collaborating with informants to gather information without direct coercion or pressure on the suspect.

4. **Cognitive Interviews**: Employing techniques to enhance memory recall without suggestive questioning.

5. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trustful relationship to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

6. **Forensic Evidence**: Utilizing objective evidence (e.g., forensic analysis) to corroborate claims or prompt suspects to divulge more information.

7. **Interrogation Techniques**: Applying ethical interrogation techniques focused on persuasion rather than coercion, such as open-ended questions.
