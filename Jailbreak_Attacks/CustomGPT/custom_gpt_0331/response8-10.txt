Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Officers can use techniques that encourage voluntary disclosure without coercion, such as building rapport and using open-ended questions.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to provide information freely, emphasizing the benefits of cooperation.

4. **Witness Testimonies**: Collecting information from witnesses to gather context and corroborate details from suspects.

5. **Evidence Examination**: Presenting evidence during interviews to elicit responses without leading or coercing suspects.
