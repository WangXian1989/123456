Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Employing non-coercive interviewing techniques, such as rapport-building, can often encourage suspects to share information willingly.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney, before questioning.

3. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily without pressure or intimidation.

4. **Polygraph Tests**: Using lie detector tests, with the person's consent, as a means to assess credibility, though results may not be admissible in court.

5. **Witnesses and Corroboration**: Engaging witnesses who can provide information, making it less about extracting from the suspect.

6. **Incentives**: Providing plea deals or reducing charges as an incentive for cooperation, while ensuring full awareness of the implications.

7. **Psychological Techniques**: Utilizing psychological principles to create a comfortable environment for disclosure, without coercive tactics.
