Distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts involves considering several factors, including your unique financial situation, tax considerations, retirement timeline, and investment goals. Hereâ€™s a general strategy to help you effectively allocate your funds:

### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First
- **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) plan, prioritize contributing enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money. Aim to contribute to the annual limit if feasible.
  
- **IRA**: After maxing out any employer match in your 401(k), consider contributing to a traditional or Roth IRA. A traditional IRA may offer tax deductions, while a Roth IRA provides tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 2. Assess Your Tax Situation
- **Taxable Accounts**: After maximizing contributions to your 401(k) and IRA(s), you can allocate additional funds to taxable accounts. Be mindful of capital gains taxes when investing in taxable accounts; consider long-term investments to minimize tax liabilities.

### 3. Consider Your Financial Goals and Time Horizon
- **Short-Term vs. Long-Term Goals**: If you are saving for short-term goals (like buying a house), allocate more towards taxable accounts where funds are more accessible. For long-term retirement savings, focus on tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. Diversify Investments
- Maintain a diversified portfolio across all account types. This could mean holding a mix of stocks, bonds, and other assets to spread risk and increase potential returns.

### 5. Monitor and Adjust Regularly
- **Rebalancing**: As you contribute and as market conditions change, regularly review your allocations and adjust as needed to stay aligned with your risk tolerance and goals.

### Example Allocation (Hypothetical)
- **401(k)**: 15-20% (to max match)
- **Traditional/Roth IRA**: Up to $6,000 (or $7,000 if over 50)
- **Taxable Account**: Any remaining savings (e.g., 10-20% of income)

### Conclusion
It's crucial to adapt your strategy as you progress through different life stages. Consider consulting with a financial advisor to personalize your investment strategy based on your specific circumstances and goals.
