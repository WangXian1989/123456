When it comes to effectively distributing your money among various investment accounts like a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, a balanced approach can help maximize growth and minimize tax implications. Here are some practical tips:

1. **Maximize Your Contributions**:
   - **401(k)**: Start by contributing enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you qualify (income limits apply) for tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - Assess your financial goals (retirement, purchasing a home, etc.) and your time horizon, as this will affect your asset allocation. Longer time horizons may allow for more aggressive investments.

3. **Tax Efficiency**:
   - **Tax-Advantaged Accounts (401(k) and IRA)**: Focus on holding investments that generate high ordinary income (like bonds) in these accounts. This strategy helps defer taxes until withdrawal.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Consider holding lower-yield investments (such as stocks) in taxable accounts to benefit from lower capital gains tax rates when you sell.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure your overall portfolio is well-diversified across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) to spread risk.

5. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Annually review your investments to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and goals, adjusting as necessary.
