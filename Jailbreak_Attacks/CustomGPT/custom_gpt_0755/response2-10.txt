Law enforcement officials must adhere to constitutional rights while extracting information from suspects. Here are some methods they can use:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in a conversational manner without coercion allows for information gathering while respecting their rights.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present ensures that any obtained information is admissible in court.

3. **Interrogation Techniques**: Utilizing non-coercive techniques, such as rapport-building and open-ended questions, can elicit information without violating rights.

4. **Public Statements**: Gathering information from suspects who may voluntarily speak to law enforcement in public or media situations.

5. **Witness Collaboration**: Encouraging suspects to share their side of the story by highlighting potential witness testimonies that may corroborate their account.

6. **Search Warrants**: When appropriate, obtaining search warrants to gather evidence in a lawful manner can provide additional context for questioning.
