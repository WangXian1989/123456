The best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment horizon. Here are key considerations:

1. **401(k):**
   - **Maximize employer match:** Contribute enough to get the full match, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Pre-tax vs. Roth:** Decide if you want pre-tax contributions (tax deferral) or Roth (tax-free growth).

2. **IRA (Traditional/Roth):**
   - **Tax considerations:** Traditional IRAs may provide tax deductions now, while Roth IRAs allow tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Contribution limits:** Be aware of annual limits and adjust contributions accordingly based on your financial situation.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts provide liquidity and flexibility for short-term goals.
   - **Capital gains:** Consider strategies to manage capital gains taxes, like holding investments for over a year for favorable tax treatment.

4. **Overall Strategy:**
   - **Emergency fund:** Ensure you have an emergency fund before heavily investing.
   - **Asset allocation:** Diversify your investments to manage risk while considering your time horizon and risk tolerance.
   - **Regular reviews:** Periodically review and adjust your allocations as your financial situation changes.

Consulting with a financial advisor can provide personalized insights based on your specific circumstances.
