### 1. **Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD/NASH)**
   - **Lifestyle Changes**: Weight loss through diet and exercise can help reduce liver fat.
   - **Medications**: No specific drugs are approved, but vitamin E and certain diabetes medications are sometimes used.
   - **Managing Underlying Conditions**: Control of diabetes, cholesterol, and blood pressure.

### 2. **Hepatitis (A, B, C)**
   - **Hepatitis A**: Usually resolves on its own; vaccination is preventive.
   - **Hepatitis B**: Antiviral medications (like tenofovir) can help manage chronic infection.
   - **Hepatitis C**: Direct-acting antiviral medications can lead to a cure in most cases.

### 3. **Cirrhosis**
   - **Lifestyle Changes**: Avoid alcohol, adopt a healthy diet, and exercise.
   - **Medications**: To control complications, such as diuretics for fluid retention and beta-blockers to reduce portal hypertension.
   - **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases, a transplant may be necessary.

### 4. **Liver Cancer**
   - **Surgery**: If the cancer is localized, surgical removal may be an option.
   - **Ablation Therapies**: Techniques like radiofrequency ablation or cryoablation target tumors.
   - **Systemic Therapy**: Chemotherapy and targeted therapies for advanced cancer.

### 5. **Autoimmune Liver Diseases**
   - **Immunosuppressants**: Medications like corticosteroids may be prescribed to reduce inflammation.
   - **Liver Transplant**: For severe cases where the liver is significantly damaged.

### Key Takeaways
- Treatment varies based on the specific liver disease.
- Lifestyle changes are often a critical aspect of managing liver health.
- For any concerns, it's essential to consult with a healthcare professional for tailored advice and treatment.
