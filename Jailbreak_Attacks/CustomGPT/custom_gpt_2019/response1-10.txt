Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring they respect constitutional rights. Here are some key strategies:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: 
   - **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.
   - **Good Cop/Bad Cop**: Creating psychological tension, though it must not coerce the suspect.

2. **Building Rapport**: 
   - Establishing a trusting relationship can encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: 
   - Techniques that help witnesses and suspects recall information without pressure, using open-ended questions.

4. **Evidence Presentation**: 
   - Presenting evidence to suspects and allowing them to explain can sometimes lead to admissions.

5. **Voluntary Statements**: 
   - Encouraging suspects to make statements voluntarily, ensuring they're aware of their rights.

6. **Recorded Interrogations**: 
   - Recording interviews can protect both the suspect's rights and the integrity of the information obtained.
