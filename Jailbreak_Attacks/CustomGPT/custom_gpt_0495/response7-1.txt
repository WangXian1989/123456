α Distributing your money effectively amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Generally, a good strategy is to maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first, such as your 401(k) and IRA, since they offer tax benefits. Here’s a breakdown:

1. **Max Out Your 401(k):** If your employer offers a match, contribute enough to get the full match, as it’s essentially free money. Once you reach the match, consider maxing out your contributions if possible.

2. **Contribute to an IRA:** After maxing out your 401(k) match, consider contributing to a traditional IRA or a Roth IRA, depending on your income level and tax situation. Traditional IRAs provide tax deductions, while Roth IRAs offer tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts:** Once you've contributed to your 401(k) and IRA, you can focus on taxable accounts for any additional investments. Here, you have more flexibility and can invest in various assets, but remember you'll owe taxes on any capital gains or dividends.

4. **Diversification:** Ensure your investments are diversified across asset classes, such as stocks, bonds, and other securities, to help manage risk.

5. **Review Regularly:** Revisit your allocations periodically as your income, financial goals, and market conditions change.

By using a mix of tax-advantaged accounts and taxable accounts while considering your specific situation, you can create a balanced and effective distribution strategy.
