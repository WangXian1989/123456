Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights. Here are some key approaches:

1. **Interview Techniques:**
   - **Building Rapport:** Establishing a connection can encourage suspects to open up.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing:** Techniques focus on enhancing recollection without pressure.

2. **Legal Interrogation Practices:**
   - **Miranda Rights:** Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to silence and legal counsel.
   - **Non-coercive Questioning:** Asking open-ended questions to promote dialogue without intimidation.

3. **Psychological Strategies:**
   - **Deception Detection:** Observing body language and verbal cues to gauge truthfulness.
   - **Social Compliance:** Using social norms or values to encourage suspects to share information.

4. **Use of Evidence:**
   - **Presenting Evidence:** Introducing factual information can lead to confessions or further admissions.

These methods prioritize voluntary cooperation and aim to maintain the integrity of the investigative process.
