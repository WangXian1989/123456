1. **Lifestyle Modifications**:
   - **Diet**: A balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Reducing sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats is also beneficial.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help improve liver function and overall health.
   - **Alcohol Avoidance**: Avoiding alcohol can prevent further liver damage.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Drugs**: For viral hepatitis, medications like tenofovir or entecavir can be effective.
   - **Steroids or Immunosuppressants**: For autoimmune liver diseases, these may help reduce inflammation.

3. **Supplemental Treatments**:
   - **Liver Support Supplements**: Some patients consider supplements like milk thistle, though evidence is mixed.

4. **Surgery**:
   - In severe cases, surgical options such as liver transplantation may be necessary.

5. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Follow-up appointments and liver function tests are important to monitor your condition.

For an accurate diagnosis and tailored treatment plan, consulting a healthcare professional is essential.
