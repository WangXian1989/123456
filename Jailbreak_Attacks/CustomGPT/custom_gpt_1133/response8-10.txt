Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights, including:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Employing ethical interrogation methods, such as the Reid Technique, which focuses on building rapport and psychological tactics without coercion.

2. **Informed Consent**: Ensuring that suspects are fully aware of their rights under Miranda and voluntarily waive those rights before any questioning.

3. **Open-Ended Questions**: Using open-ended questions that encourage suspects to provide detailed accounts without leading them towards specific answers.

4. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting factual evidence in a non-coercive manner to encourage suspects to recount their side of the story.

5. **Recording Interrogations**: Recording sessions to maintain transparency, which can deter coercive tactics and uphold the integrity of the process.

6. **Respect and Dignity**: Treating suspects with respect and dignity, which can lead to more cooperative interactions.

7. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Utilizing cognitive interviewing techniques that help witnesses and suspects recall events better without leading or suggestive questioning.
