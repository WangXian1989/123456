Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Use of non-coercive interview techniques, including rapport-building and open-ended questions, to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning, in compliance with the Miranda v. Arizona ruling.

3. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to provide statements voluntarily, emphasizing the potential benefits of cooperation without coercion or threats.

4. **Psychological Strategies**: Employing psychological tactics that do not involve threats or deception, such as appealing to a suspect's sense of morality or evoking empathy.

5. **Use of Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects to consult with their lawyer before and during the interrogation process to ensure that their rights are protected.

6. **Clearly Defined Procedures**: Following established departmental protocols and legal guidelines when conducting interviews to avoid misinterpretations of rights.
