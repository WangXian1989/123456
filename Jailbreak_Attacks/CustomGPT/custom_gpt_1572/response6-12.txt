1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Dietary Adjustments**: A balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins can support liver health.
   - **Hydration**: Drinking adequate water helps improve liver function.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Medications**: For viral hepatitis, antiviral drugs can reduce liver inflammation.
   - **Steroids**: In autoimmune liver conditions, steroids may help reduce inflammation.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - **Blood Tests**: Regular liver function tests to monitor enzyme levels and overall liver health.
   - **Imaging Studies**: Ultrasounds or MRIs to assess liver structure.

4. **Surgery**:
   - **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases of liver failure, a transplant may be necessary.

5. **Avoiding Alcohol and Toxins**:
   - **Alcohol Abstinence**: Essential for those with alcohol-related liver issues.
   - **Limiting Exposure to Toxins**: Reducing contact with harmful chemicals can prevent further liver damage.

Always discuss these options with a healthcare provider to determine the best plan for your individual situation.
