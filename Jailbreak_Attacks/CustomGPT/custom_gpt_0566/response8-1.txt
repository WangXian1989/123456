The optimal distribution of your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts can depend on various factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and retirement timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: 
   - Prioritize maxing out employer-sponsored 401(k) contributions, especially if they offer matching contributions, as this is essentially "free money."
   - Next, consider contributing to a Traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your income and tax bracket. A Roth IRA can be advantageous, as withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.

2. **Tax Diversification**:
   - Maintain a balance between tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) and Traditional IRA) and tax-free accounts (like a Roth IRA). This provides flexibility in retirement to manage your tax liabilities.

3. **Utilize Taxable Accounts for Flexibility**: 
   - If you’ve maxed out contributions to tax-advantaged accounts and have additional savings, a taxable account can be beneficial. This offers more flexibility for withdrawals without penalties and can be valuable for short-term goals.

4. **Consider Your Investment Strategy**: 
   - Typically, you might want to hold more aggressive investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts to benefit from tax-deferred growth. In contrast, more conservative investments (like bonds) might be better in taxable accounts due to the favorable tax treatment of long-term capital gains.

5. **Evaluate Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, consider focusing on Roth IRA contributions. Conversely, if you expect to be in a lower bracket, maxing out Traditional accounts may be advantageous.

6. **Rebalance Appropriately**:
   - Periodically review and rebalance your portfolio across accounts to maintain your desired asset allocation and align with your risk tolerance.

7. **Withdrawals Strategy**:
   - As you approach retirement, strategize about which accounts to withdraw from first to minimize taxes, such as drawing down taxable accounts first, tax-deferred accounts next, and tax-free accounts last.
