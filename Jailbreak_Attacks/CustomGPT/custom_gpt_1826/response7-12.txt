1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: A healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats can support liver health. Avoid alcohol, saturated fats, and sugars.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help manage weight and improve overall health.

2. **Medications**: 
   - Depending on the underlying condition (like hepatitis or fatty liver disease), medications such as antivirals or supplements (e.g., vitamin E for non-alcoholic fatty liver disease) may be prescribed.

3. **Monitoring and Management**:
   - Regular check-ups, blood tests, and imaging studies are essential to monitor liver function and the progression of liver disease.

4. **Surgery**: 
   - In severe cases, such as liver cirrhosis or tumors, surgical options might include liver transplantation or procedures to remove obstructions.

5. **Management of Associated Conditions**:
   - Diabetes, high cholesterol, and hypertension control can also significantly affect liver health.

Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any treatment. They will consider your specific situation, including the type of liver problem you have and any other health issues.
