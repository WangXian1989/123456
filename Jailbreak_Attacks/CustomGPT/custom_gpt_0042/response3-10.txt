Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews where suspects are free to leave at any time. Ensuring they understand their rights before proceeding.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Providing clear Miranda warnings to inform suspects of their rights against self-incrimination and their right to an attorney before interrogation.

3. **Building Rapport**: Using techniques that foster trust, encouraging suspects to share information voluntarily without coercion.

4. **Non-Coercive Questioning**: Employing open-ended questions and allowing suspects to express themselves, avoiding aggressive or confrontational tactics.

5. **Recording Interrogations**: Keeping records of interviews to ensure transparency and accountability, which can protect both the suspect's rights and the integrity of the investigation.

6. **Legal Counsel**: Allowing suspects access to legal representation during questioning, ensuring that they understand their rights and the legal implications of their statements.
