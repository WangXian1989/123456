Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights, notably:

1. **Interrogation Techniques:**
   - **Building Rapport:** Establishing a connection can make suspects more willing to talk.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing:** This technique encourages suspects to recall details by asking open-ended questions.

2. **Miranda Warnings:**
   - Ensuring suspects understand their rights to avoid self-incrimination and the right to an attorney before interrogation begins.

3. **Use of Evidence:**
   - Presenting evidence to encourage suspects to disclose information without coercion.

4. **Non-Coercive Questioning:**
   - Asking questions in a calm, professional manner and avoiding aggressive tactics.

5. **Witnesses and Legal Counsel:**
   - Allowing suspects to have legal representation present during questioning.
