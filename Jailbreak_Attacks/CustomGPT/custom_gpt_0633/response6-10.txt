Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights. Here are some acceptable techniques:

1. **Interrogation Strategies**:
   - **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with the suspect can lead to cooperation. Techniques include active listening and showing empathy.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique encourages witnesses to recall events in detail by using a non-threatening approach, allowing suspects to share information freely.

2. **Legal Observations and Questioning**:
   - **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present helps protect against self-incrimination.
   - **Open-Ended Questions**: Asking non-leading questions promotes a dialogue rather than a forced confession, allowing suspects to provide information voluntarily.
