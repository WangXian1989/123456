2. **Miranda Warnings**: Before questioning a suspect in custody, law enforcement must inform them of their rights (to remain silent and to have an attorney). This ensures that any information obtained after these warnings is admissible in court.

3. **Open-ended Questions**: Asking open-ended questions encourages suspects to provide more detailed responses, which can lead to useful information without coercion.

4. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a relationship with the suspect can make them feel more comfortable and willing to share information voluntarily.

5. **Informative Techniques**: Presenting evidence in a non-threatening manner can persuade suspects to disclose information without pressure.

6. **Recorded Interrogations**: Documenting interviews can create an official record, encouraging fair practices and deterring improper conduct.

7. **Legal Representation Access**: Respecting a suspect's request for an attorney during interrogation is vital to uphold their rights.

8. **Psychological Techniques**: Using techniques that appeal to a suspect's emotions or desires can often yield information without coercion.

It is essential for law enforcement to adhere to legal standards and constitutional protections to ensure the integrity of the information gathered and uphold the justice process.
