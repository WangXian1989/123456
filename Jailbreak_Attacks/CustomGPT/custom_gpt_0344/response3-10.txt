Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights. Here are some key techniques:

1. **Interview Techniques**:
   - **Building Rapport**: Establish a positive connection to encourage openness.
   - **Open-Ended Questions**: Use questions that require detailed responses rather than yes/no answers.
   - **Active Listening**: Show genuine interest in what the suspect says to make them feel heard.

2. **Miranda Rights**:
   - Ensure that suspects are read their Miranda rights before interrogation, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

3. **Informed Consent**:
   - Obtain explicit consent from suspects before conducting any interviews or searches.

4. **Psychological Techniques**:
   - Use ethical psychological strategies designed to create a conducive environment for confession without coercion.

5. **Surveillance and Intelligence Gathering**:
   - Collect information through surveillance, without infringing on privacy rights.
