### 1. Prioritize Contributions
- **401(k)**: Maximize employer match first. This is essentially free money.
- **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you're eligible, as it offers tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement, or a Traditional IRA for tax deductions now.
- **Taxable Accounts**: After maxing out retirement accounts, use taxable accounts for additional savings or investments.

### 2. Consider Tax Implications
- **401(k) and IRA**: Contributions are often tax-deferred. Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
- **Roth Options**: Contributions are made post-tax, but withdrawals are tax-free.
- **Taxable Accounts**: You'll pay capital gains taxes on profits when you sell investments, so consider holding investments longer to minimize these taxes.

### 3. Asset Allocation
- **401(k)**: Invest primarily in stock and bond funds suited for your age and risk tolerance, taking advantage of the investment options your plan offers.
- **IRA**: Similar allocation approach, but you can have more flexibility in choosing individual stocks, bonds, or alternative investments compared to a 401(k).
- **Taxable Accounts**: Focus on tax-efficient investments (like index funds or ETFs) that tend to yield lower capital gains distributions, and consider municipal bonds for tax-free income.

### 4. Rebalance and Monitor
Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on market performance, changes in your risk tolerance, and near-term financial needs.

### 5. Consult a Financial Advisor
Consider speaking with a professional who can provide personalized advice tailored to your financial situation, tax considerations, and long-term goals.

By strategically managing contributions and considering tax implications, you can optimize the benefits of each account type.
