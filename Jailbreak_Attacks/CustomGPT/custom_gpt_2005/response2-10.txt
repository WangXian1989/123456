3. **Interviewing**: Using non-coercive interviewing techniques that focus on establishing trust and understanding the suspect's perspective, rather than using aggressive tactics.

4. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to provide information or confessions willingly, without pressure or coercion, by explaining the benefits of cooperation.

5. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting clear evidence to the suspect to provoke a reaction or admission, while ensuring the conversation remains voluntary and non-threatening.
