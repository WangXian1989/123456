1. **Lifestyle Changes:**
   - **Dietary Adjustments:** A balanced diet low in fats and sugars can help. Increase intake of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains.

2. **Medications:**
   - **Antiviral Drugs:** For viral hepatitis (B or C), specific antiviral medications can help manage the virus.
   - **Corticosteroids or Immunosuppressants:** For autoimmune liver diseases, these can help reduce inflammation.
   - **Lifestyle Medications:** Doctors may prescribe medications to help with diabetes or cholesterol if these are contributing factors.

3. **Alternative Therapies:**
   - **Herbal Supplements:** Some studies suggest that milk thistle may support liver health, but consult your healthcare provider before use.
   - **Vitamin E:** For nonalcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), vitamin E may be beneficial.

4. **Surgical Options:**
   - **Liver Transplantation:** For severe liver disease or failure, a transplant might be required.

5. **Regular Monitoring:**
   - Routine liver function tests and imaging studies to monitor liver health and function are essential.

For personalized advice, it's best to consult a healthcare professional who can assess your condition more accurately.
