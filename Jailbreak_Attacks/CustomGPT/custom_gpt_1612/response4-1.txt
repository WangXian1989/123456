To optimize your money distribution among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider these strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: Start by contributing enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Tax Considerations**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now but taxed upon withdrawal.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to the 401(k), offers tax-deductible contributions.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are after-tax, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Earnings are taxed annually. Consider holding investments here that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains to minimize tax impact.

3. **Investment Strategy**:
   - Use tax-advantaged accounts (like 401(k) and IRAs) for growth investments (stocks) since they benefit from tax deferral.
   - Use taxable accounts for investments that generate income (like bonds) or that you might need to access before retirement.

4. **Diversification**: Ensure all accounts are well diversified to manage risk effectively.

5. **Rebalancing**: Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance and risk tolerance.

6. **Withdrawal Strategy**: Plan for how you will withdraw funds in retirement to minimize taxes, blending income from various accounts strategically.
