When considering the best way to distribute your money amongst your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's important to take into account several factors including your current financial situation, tax implications, retirement goals, and investment preferences. Here are some general guidelines to help you make a decision:

1. **Maximize Contributions to Tax-Advantaged Accounts**:
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a 401(k) match, contribute enough to get the full match first. This is essentially free money.
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Traditional IRA or a Roth IRA based on your income level and tax situation. Roth IRAs are funded with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

2. **Consider Your Tax Bracket**:
   - If you're in a higher tax bracket now and expect to be in a lower one in retirement, a Traditional IRA and 401(k) could be beneficial as contributions are tax-deductible.
   - If you anticipate being in the same or higher tax bracket in retirement, a Roth IRA may be the better option for tax-free withdrawals.

3. **Diversification of Investments**:
   - Within these accounts, diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.) to reduce risk.

4. **Liquidity Needs**:
   - Taxable accounts should be used for money that you may need before retirement, as they allow for easier access to funds without penalities.

5. **Long-Term vs Short-Term Goals**:
   - Allocate funds based on when you expect to need access to them. Long-term savings can go into retirement accounts, whereas shorter-term goals may be better suited for taxable accounts.

6. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review and rebalance your investment strategy to stay aligned with your financial goals and risk tolerance.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you're unsure or have complex financial situations, consider seeking advice from a financial advisor who can provide personalized guidance.
