1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: A balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins can support liver health. Avoiding high-fat, high-sugar, and processed foods is also recommended.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help maintain a healthy weight and reduce the risk of liver disease.

2. **Medication**:
   - Depending on the specific liver condition (like hepatitis, cirrhosis, or fatty liver disease), medication may be prescribed. This could include antiviral drugs for hepatitis or medications to manage symptoms.

3. **Limit Alcohol Intake**:
   - Reducing or eliminating alcohol consumption can help prevent further liver damage, especially if you have alcoholic liver disease.

4. **Managing Underlying Conditions**:
   - Conditions like diabetes, high cholesterol, or obesity can affect liver health. Managing these through medication and lifestyle changes may improve liver function.

5. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Frequent check-ups and liver function tests can help monitor your condition and make necessary adjustments to your treatment plan.

6. **Possible Interventions**:
   - In advanced cases, treatments like liver transplantation may be considered. This is usually a last resort for severe liver failure.
