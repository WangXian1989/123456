When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, several factors come into play, including your retirement goals, tax situation, time horizon, and investment strategy. Here are some general guidelines to help you develop an effective strategy:

1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**: 
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution to your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of this match. It's essentially "free money" for your retirement.

2. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**:
   - After getting the full employer match, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth, depending on your tax situation) since these accounts often have tax advantages that can benefit your long-term savings.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - **Traditional 401(k) and IRA**: Contributions reduce your taxable income in the year you contribute but are taxed when you withdraw in retirement.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Earnings from investments in these accounts can be subject to capital gains tax, so consider utilizing tax-efficient investments here (like index funds and ETFs).

4. **Evaluate Your Investment Goals and Time Horizon**:
   - If you have a long time until retirement, you might want to allocate a higher percentage of your contributions to growth-oriented investments within your 401(k) and IRA.
   - If you're closer to retirement, consider gradually shifting your investments toward more conservative assets to preserve capital.

5. **Diversification Across Accounts**:
   - Aim for a diversified investment strategy, ensuring that you are not overly concentrated in one type of investment across all accounts. This can help manage risk effectively.

6. **Regularly Review and Rebalance**:
   - Monitor your account performances and contributions regularly, rebalancing as necessary to align with your risk tolerance and financial goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - Consider seeking professional advice tailored to your specific financial situation and goals. They can provide personalized strategies based on your needs.
