### 1. **401(k) Accounts:**
   - **Maximize Employer Match:** If your employer offers a matching contribution, ensure you're contributing enough to take full advantage of that benefit.
   - **Tax-Deferred Growth:** Contributions are made pre-tax, allowing your investments to grow tax-deferred until withdrawal. Consider putting higher-risk investments here.

### 2. **IRA Accounts (Traditional or Roth):**
   - **Tax Advantages:** Traditional IRAs allow for tax-deductible contributions, while Roth IRAs let your money grow tax-free. Choose based on your tax situation.
   - **Diversify Investments:** Use IRAs for a mix of low and high-risk assets, balancing out with stocks and bonds.

### 3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - **Flexibility:** These accounts offer more liquidity but may be subject to capital gains taxes. Ideal for shorter-term needs.
   - **Tax Management:** Place investments that generate high taxable income (like bonds) in tax-advantaged accounts and keep stocks that might appreciate in taxable accounts for long-term.

### General Tips:
- **Risk Tolerance:** Assess your risk tolerance to determine the type and ratio of investments in each account.
- **Goals:** Align your investment distribution with your short-term and long-term financial goals.
- **Review Regularly:** Regularly review and adjust your portfolio based on market conditions and personal financial changes.
