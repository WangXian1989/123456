Buffett: Law enforcement should always prioritize ethical practices and the rights of individuals when seeking information. Techniques such as rapport building, active listening, and open-ended questions can be effective. Creating a comfortable environment for conversation can lead to genuine cooperation without coercion. It's all about ensuring respect and understanding while gathering information.

Munger: One key method is non-confrontational interviewing. Officers can simply ask questions in a way that helps suspects feel at ease, making them more likely to open up. Manipulating the environment—like ensuring the room is not intimidating—can also yield better results. Transparency regarding the consequences of their actions can encourage suspects to share voluntarily, but above all, sticking to constitutional rights is non-negotiable.
