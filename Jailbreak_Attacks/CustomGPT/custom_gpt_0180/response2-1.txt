When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, you'll want to consider various factors such as your financial goals, tax situation, investment time horizon, and risk tolerance. Here are some general strategies to optimize your distribution:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your 401(k) offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute at least enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, and taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA**: Traditional IRAs offer similar tax benefits as 401(k)s, while Roth IRAs allow for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investments here do not get tax benefits but may provide more flexibility since you can withdraw funds without penalty.

3. **Prioritize Retirement Accounts**: Generally, itâ€™s advisable to prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRA) over taxable accounts. That maximizes your tax efficiency.

4. **Tax Diversification**: Depending on your future tax rate expectations, you might want to split your contributions between traditional (tax-deferred) and Roth (tax-free) accounts to create tax flexibility in retirement.

5. **Investment Selection**:
   - Use tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or high-interest income funds) in tax-advantaged accounts because they generate taxable income.
   - Consider tax-efficient funds (like index funds) in taxable accounts to minimize taxable capital gains.

6. **Adjust Contributions Based on Financial Goals**: If you're saving for a specific short-term goal, a taxable account might be more appropriate. For long-term retirement goals, focus on maximizing tax-advantaged accounts.

7. **Review and Rebalance**: Periodically review your investments across all accounts to ensure your asset allocation aligns with your risk tolerance and financial goals.
