Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Officers can conduct interviews where suspects are not coerced and feel free to leave. Building rapport can encourage suspects to share information willingly.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Prior to interrogating a suspect in custody, officers must issue Miranda warnings, informing them of their right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

3. **Recording Interrogations**: By recording interviews, law enforcement can ensure transparency and accountability, reducing claims of coercion.

4. **Use of Open-Ended Questions**: Asking open-ended questions allows suspects to provide information in their own words, fostering a more open dialogue.

5. **Cognitive Interview Techniques**: This method focuses on helping the suspect recall details through guided visualization and context reinstatement, without pressure.

6. **Incentives**: Offering plea bargains or other incentives can encourage cooperation without coercion.

7. **Psychological Techniques**: Officers may use non-confrontational techniques to appeal to emotions or logic, promoting voluntary disclosure.
