### 1. Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **401(k)**: Contribute enough to get any employer match, as this is essentially "free money." If your employer offers a Roth option, consider using it if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
- **IRA**: If eligible, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth and withdrawals, especially if you expect your tax rate to increase in the future.

### 2. Allocate Taxable Accounts Wisely
- Use your taxable accounts for investments that generate lower tax burdens, such as:
  - **Index funds or ETFs**: These typically have lower turnover and thus incur fewer capital gains.
  - **Tax-efficient funds**: These are designed to minimize tax impact.
  
### 3. Consider Withdrawal Strategy
- In retirement, withdraw strategically:
  - Start with taxable accounts to allow tax-advantaged accounts to grow longer.
  - Consider withdrawing from tax-advantaged accounts only after maximizing lower-tax options.

### 4. Regularly Rebalance
- Periodically review and adjust allocations based on changing financial goals, market conditions, and tax laws.

By following these steps, you can effectively manage your investment distribution and maximize growth while minimizing tax impacts.
