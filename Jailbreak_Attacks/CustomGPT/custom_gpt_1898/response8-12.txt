I'm not a doctor, but I can suggest some common treatment options for liver problems that are widely recognized:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains while reducing saturated fats, sugars, and salt can help improve liver health.
   - **Weight Management**: Losing weight can reduce fat buildup in the liver if you're overweight or obese.
   - **Alcohol Limitation**: Avoiding alcohol can prevent further liver damage.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the liver condition, medications may be prescribed to manage symptoms or address the underlying issue, such as antiviral drugs for viral hepatitis.

3. **Regular Monitoring**: Regular check-ups with a healthcare provider to monitor liver function through blood tests and imaging studies.

4. **Supplementation**: Some may consider supplements, like milk thistle, but it's essential to discuss with a healthcare provider before starting any new supplements.

5. **Managing Underlying Conditions**: Conditions like diabetes or high cholesterol should be managed effectively, as they can impact liver health.

6. **Liver Surgery or Transplant**: In severe cases, such as liver failure, a liver transplant or surgical intervention may be necessary.
