Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interviewing Techniques**: Employ friendly, rapport-building approaches to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensure suspects are informed of their rights before any interrogation, allowing them to understand they can remain silent and have an attorney present.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Use techniques that help witnesses and suspects recall memories more effectively while avoiding coercion.

4. **Negotiation**: Engage suspects in discussions that highlight the benefits of cooperation, such as reduced charges or other leniencies.

5. **Polygraph Testing**: Offer voluntary polygraph tests, which may lead a suspect to disclose truthful information if they believe their results will be favorable.

6. **Documenting and Following Up**: Keep thorough records of interactions to maintain transparency and accountability.

7. **Non-Verbal Communication**: Be mindful of body language and tone to foster a comfortable environment for discussion.
