When distributing your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies for optimal growth and tax efficiency:

### 1. **Maximize Employer-Sponsored Plans**
   - **401(k) Contributions**: If your employer offers matching contributions, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially free money that boosts your retirement savings.

### 2. **Utilize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Traditional IRA**: 
     - **Pre-tax Contributions**: These can reduce your taxable income now, deferring taxes until withdrawal.
     - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of annual limits; for 2023, it's $6,500 ($7,500 if age 50 or older).
   - **Roth IRA**: 
     - Consider contributing if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, but all withdrawals in retirement are tax-free.
     - **Income Limits**: Check eligibility for contributions based on your income.

### 3. **Consider Taxable Investment Accounts**
   - Use these accounts for investments that may generate taxable income or that you may want to access before retirement age without penalties.
   - Consider holding **low-turnover funds** or **index funds** to minimize capital gains taxes.
   - **Tax-efficient investments**: Municipal bonds or certain types of ETFs can yield favorable tax treatment.

### 4. **Asset Allocation Approach**
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Your investment strategy should reflect your risk tolerance. Growth-oriented investments might be better suited for tax-deferred accounts, as they can benefit from long-term capital gains.
   - **Asset Location Strategy**: 
     - **Bonds**: Place income-producing investments, like bonds or REITs, in tax-advantaged accounts to shield interest income from taxes.
     - **Stocks**: Consider placing higher-growth investments in taxable accounts since long-term capital gains are usually taxed at a lower rate than short-term income.

### 5. **Roth Conversions**
   - If you have the ability, consider performing a Roth conversion in lower income years. This can set you up for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

### 6. **Annual Review and Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review and adjust your allocations based on performance, changing financial goals, and life circumstances.
