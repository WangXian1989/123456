### 3. **Performance Monitoring**
   - **Activity Tracking**: Use biometric data to analyze work patterns. For instance, if using smart devices, you can monitor time spent on tasks and engagement levels.
   - **Wearable Technology**: If applicable, consider providing employees with wearables that track their performance metrics (e.g., productivity levels based on movements or tasks completed).

### 4. **Data Analytics**
   - **Employee Dashboards**: Create dashboards that visualize attendance and performance data over time, helping managers identify trends and areas needing improvement.

### 5. **Compliance and Ethics**
   - **Privacy Policies**: Make sure to inform employees about the biometric data collection process. Obtain consent and provide options for opting out if necessary.
   - **Data Security**: Ensure that all biometric data is stored securely and complies with local laws (like GDPR). Regularly review data management policies.

### 6. **Training and Support**
   - **Employee Training**: Offer training sessions to explain how these tools work, their purpose, and benefits for both the company and employee productivity.
   - **Support Systems**: Provide support for employees who may have concerns about privacy or who encounter issues with the technology.
