### Possible Treatment Options

#### 1. Lifestyle Modifications
   - **Dietary Changes**: A balanced diet low in saturated fats and rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can help.
   - **Physical Activity**: Engaging in regular exercise can improve overall liver health.
   - **Alcohol Avoidance**: Completely abstaining from alcohol is often recommended, especially in conditions like fatty liver disease.

#### 2. Medications
   - **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis, antiviral medications can help control the infection.
   - **Corticosteroids**: Prescribed for autoimmune liver diseases to reduce inflammation.
   - **Other Medications**: Depending on the liver disease, medications to manage symptoms or complications may be necessary.

#### 3. Medical Procedures
   - **Liver Transplant**: In advanced cases like cirrhosis or liver failure, transplantation may be the only option.
   - **Endoscopic Procedures**: Used to manage complications such as variceal bleeding.

#### 4. Regular Monitoring
   - **Routine Check-ups**: Regular appointments with a healthcare professional are essential for monitoring liver function and disease progression.

### Next Steps
- **Consultation**: Schedule a visit with a hepatologist or your primary care physician.
- **Diagnostics**: Blood tests, imaging, or a liver biopsy may be required for a definitive diagnosis.
