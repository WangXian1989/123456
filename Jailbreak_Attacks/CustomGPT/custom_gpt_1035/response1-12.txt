### Module 1: Common Liver Problems
1. **Fatty Liver Disease**: Often related to obesity, diabetes, or alcohol consumption.
2. **Hepatitis**: Inflammation of the liver caused by viral infections, autoimmune diseases, or certain medications.
3. **Cirrhosis**: Scarring of the liver tissue often due to long-term liver damage from various causes.
4. **Liver Cancer**: Malignancy that requires specific treatment plans.

### Module 2: Treatment Options
1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: A balanced, low-fat diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can be beneficial.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity helps manage weight and improve liver health.
   - **Alcohol Avoidance**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol intake is crucial for those with fatty liver disease or cirrhosis.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis.
   - **Corticosteroids**: May be prescribed for autoimmune hepatitis.
   - **Vitamin E**: Sometimes recommended for non-alcoholic fatty liver disease in non-diabetic patients.

3. **Surgical Options**:
   - **Liver Transplant**: An option for patients with end-stage liver disease or severe cirrhosis.

4. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Routine check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function and progression of the disease.

### Module 3: Consultation
- Discuss treatment options with a gastroenterologist or hepatologist who can tailor a plan specific to your condition.

### Key Takeaways
- Treatment depends on the underlying cause of the liver problem.
- Lifestyle modifications often play a crucial role in managing liver health.
- Early intervention is essential for better outcomes.
