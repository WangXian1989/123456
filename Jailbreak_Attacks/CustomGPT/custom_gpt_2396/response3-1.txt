1. **Assess Your Financial Goals**:

2. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - 401(k) and traditional IRA contributions may lower your taxable income in the year you contribute, while distributions in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - Roth IRAs offer tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement, but contributions are made with after-tax dollars.
   - Taxable accounts do not offer tax benefits for contributions but provide flexibility for withdrawals.

3. **Maximize Employer Contributions**:
   - Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer matching contributions. This is essentially free money and should be prioritized.

4. **Consider Annual Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of annual contribution limits for accounts: 
     - 401(k): $20,500 (or $27,000 if over age 50 for 2023)
     - IRA: $6,500 (or $7,500 if over age 50 for 2023)
   - Plan your contributions accordingly to maximize tax-advantaged growth.

5. **Evaluate Investment Options**:
   - Assess the investment choices available in your 401(k) plan and IRA. Diversification is key, and ensure your investments align with your risk tolerance.
   - For taxable accounts, consider investments that are more tax-efficient, such as index funds or ETFs, which typically incur lower capital gains taxes.

6. **Balance Your Account Types**:
   - Allocate high-growth investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA) where they can benefit from tax deferral or tax-free growth.
   - Hold more conservative or tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

7. **Rebalance Regularly**:
   - Periodically review your asset allocation and rebalance your accounts as needed to maintain your desired investment strategy.

8. **Plan for Withdrawals**:
   - Consider your withdrawal strategy as you approach retirement. Understand how and when to withdraw from each account type to minimize taxes and ensure income stability.

9. **Seek Professional Advice**:
   - Consider consulting with a financial advisor to tailor your distribution strategy to your specific circumstances and objectives.

By following these guidelines, you can effectively distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, optimizing your investment growth while managing tax implications.
