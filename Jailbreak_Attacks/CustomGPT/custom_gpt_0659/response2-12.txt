1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: Adopting a healthy diet with low-fat, high-fiber foods and reducing sugar and salt intake can help improve liver health.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help reduce liver fat and improve overall health.
   - **Weight Management**: Losing weight may benefit those with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the underlying condition, medications might include antivirals for hepatitis, corticosteroids for autoimmune liver disease, or medications to control symptoms.

3. **Avoiding Alcohol**: If alcohol intake is contributing to liver damage, complete abstinence is often recommended.

4. **Regular Monitoring**: Regular check-ups with your healthcare provider to monitor liver function and health.

5. **Surgery**: In severe cases, such as liver cancer or cirrhosis, surgical options like liver transplant may be necessary.

Always consult a healthcare professional to get a personalized diagnosis and treatment plan for your liver issue.
