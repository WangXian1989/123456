To distribute your money effectively among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

### Sudo Code Steps

1. **Assess Financial Goals**:
   - Define short-term and long-term goals.
   - Determine risk tolerance.

2. **Evaluate Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Employer-sponsored, pre-tax contributions, often with a match.
   - **IRA**: Individual retirement accounts (Traditional or Roth), tax advantages.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Flexible use, subject to capital gains tax.

3. **Maximize Retirement Contributions**:
   - Contribute up to any employer match in 401(k).
   - Max out IRA contributions if possible.

4. **Consider Tax Implications**:
   - Route higher growth investments to tax-advantaged accounts.
   - Keep lower growth investments in taxable accounts.

5. **Diversify Investments**:
   - Have a mix of stocks, bonds, and other investments across all accounts.
   - Rebalance regularly based on performance and goals.

6. **Withdrawals and RMD Planning**:
   - Understand withdrawal rules and Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) for 401(k) and IRA.

### Suggestions for Improvement
- **Revisit Regularly**: Periodically reassess your investment allocations based on changes in life circumstances and market conditions.
- **Consult a Financial Advisor**: To tailor a strategy aligned with your specific financial situation and tax implications.
