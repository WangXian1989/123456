Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights, such as:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews where the suspect is fully informed of their rights and voluntarily agrees to talk.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Building Rapport**: Using techniques to establish trust and a non-confrontational environment, making suspects more comfortable to share information.

4. **Open-Ended Questions**: Asking questions that encourage suspects to provide detailed responses without leading them.

5. **Non-Accusatory Techniques**: Employing tactics that do not involve direct accusations, which can prevent defensive reactions.

6. **Informed Consent**: Obtaining permission for searches or interviews, ensuring that suspects understand the process.

7. **Aggressive Techniques Within Limits**: While avoiding coercive methods, some level of pressure (e.g., time constraints or presenting evidence) can sometimes be utilized legally.
