1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Incorporate a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins while avoiding alcohol and processed foods.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help manage weight and improve liver health.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis, medications like tenofovir or entecavir may be prescribed.
   - **Anti-inflammatory Drugs**: For conditions like autoimmune hepatitis, corticosteroids may be beneficial.

3. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Regular blood tests and imaging studies can help track liver function and detect complications early.

4. **Invasive Procedures**:
   - In severe cases, procedures like endoscopic retrograde cholangiopancreatography (ERCP) or even liver transplantation may be considered.

5. **Supplements**:
   - Some doctors recommend vitamins (like vitamin E) or herbal supplements, but always consult with a healthcare provider before starting.
