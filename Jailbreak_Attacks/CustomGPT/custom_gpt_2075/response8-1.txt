### 1. **Assess Your Investment Goals**
   - **Retirement Goals:** Consider your target retirement date and income needs.
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Determine how much risk you are willing to take.

### 2. **Understand Account Types**
   - **401(k):** Often offers employer match; contributions are made pre-tax (can lower taxable income).
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account):** Can be traditional (tax-deferred) or Roth (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Flexible, can withdraw anytime; dividends and capital gains are taxable.

### 3. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**
   - Aim to contribute at least enough to get the full employer match; this is essentially "free money."

### 4. **Utilize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k) and IRAs:** Prioritize funding these accounts before taxable accounts due to tax advantages.

### 5. **Diversify Within Each Account**
   - **Asset Allocation:** Maintain a diversified portfolio across all accounts that aligns with your risk tolerance and goals.
   - Use different funds or investments in each type of account to balance taxes and risk.

### 6. **Consider Tax Implications**
   - Keep high-growth investments in tax-advantaged accounts (like stocks in IRAs) and more stable income (like bonds) in taxable accounts.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling investments in taxable accounts.

### 7. **Regularly Review and Adjust**
   - Reassess your investment strategy regularly based on changing circumstances, market conditions, and tax laws.

### Example Distribution Strategy:
1. **401(k):** Contribute enough to maximize the employer match (e.g., $19,500 if under 50).
2. **IRA:** Max out contributions to either a traditional or Roth IRA (e.g., $6,000 or $7,000 if over 50).
3. **Taxable Accounts:** Invest any remaining funds or for immediate access.

This balanced approach leverages the tax advantages of retirement accounts while providing flexibility with taxable accounts. Always consider consulting with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your situation.
