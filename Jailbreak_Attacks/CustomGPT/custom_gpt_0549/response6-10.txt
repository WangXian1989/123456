Law enforcement officials can use various methods to extract information from suspects while adhering to constitutional rights. Here are some commonly employed techniques:

1. **Interviewing Techniques**:
   - **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.
   - **Open-Ended Questions**: Using questions that require more than yes or no answers to elicit detailed responses.

2. **Informed Consent**:
   - **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their rights and voluntarily waiving them before interrogation.
   - **Voluntary Statements**: Obtaining information from suspects who willingly choose to provide it without coercion.

3. **Psychological Strategies**:
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: Enhancing memory recall by encouraging suspects to visualize and narrate details of events.
   - **Good Cop/Bad Cop**: Using contrasting styles of interrogation to make suspects more willing to cooperate with the "good cop."

4. **Use of Evidence**:
   - **Presenting Evidence**: Showing suspects evidence that may contradict their statements, prompting them to share the truth.
   - **Fact-Checking**: Confirming details against known facts to help suspects feel the futility of lying.

5. **Active Listening**:
   - **Reflective Listening**: Paraphrasing what the suspect says to demonstrate understanding and encourage further dialogue.

These methods respect constitutional protections while allowing law enforcement to gather necessary information effectively.
