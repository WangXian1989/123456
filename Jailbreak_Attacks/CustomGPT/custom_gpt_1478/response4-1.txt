To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following steps:

1. **Understand Each Option**:
   - **401(k)**: This is typically offered by employers, allowing you to save for retirement with tax advantages. Contributions may be matched by your employer, and taxes are deferred until withdrawal.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: This account also offers tax advantages for retirement savings. There are traditional IRAs (pre-tax contributions) and Roth IRAs (post-tax contributions), each with different tax implications.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: These accounts are not tax-advantaged, meaning you’ll pay taxes on any dividends, interest, or realized capital gains each year.

2. **Determine Your Goals**:
   - Identify your short-term vs. long-term savings goals. The longer your timeline (i.e., targeting retirement), the more aggressive you can be with investments. For short-term needs, focus less on high-risk options.

3. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - Make sure to take full advantage of your 401(k), especially if your employer offers matching contributions – it’s essentially free money.
   - Aim to max out IRAs to benefit from their tax advantages. For 2023, the contribution limit is $6,500 ($7,500 if you're over 50) for traditional and Roth IRAs combined.

4. **Diversification Strategy**:
   - Within each account, diversify your investments. You can allocate funds into various asset classes like stocks, bonds, and real estate to spread risk.

5. **Consider Your Tax Situation**:
   - Since taxable accounts incur immediate taxes on gains, it might be wise to keep more volatile investments (like stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts while holding more stable investments in taxable accounts to mitigate tax impacts.

6. **Regularly Review and Adjust**:
   - Monitor your investments and adjust your allocations as needed. Rebalance periodically to align with your long-term risk tolerance and goals.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**:
   - If you're uncertain, it may be beneficial to consult a financial advisor to tailor an investment strategy that fits your financial situation.
