Law enforcement officials can employ several lawful methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: 
   - Use rapport-building strategies to create a comfortable environment.
   - Employ open-ended questions that encourage dialogue and cooperation.
   - Utilize the Reid Technique, which focuses on behavioral analysis to detect deception while remaining ethical.

2. **Miranda Rights**:
   - Ensure suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning. This protects against self-incrimination.

3. **Voluntary Statements**:
   - Encourage suspects to provide information voluntarily without coercion or manipulation, ensuring they understand they can refuse to answer questions.

4. **Psychological Tactics**:
   - Apply non-coercive psychological strategies, such as cognitive dissonance, where the suspect's own statements or evidence presented create internal conflict, potentially encouraging them to confess.

5. **Use of Evidence**:
   - Present compelling evidence or witness accounts to prompt suspects to provide information, while avoiding misleading or deceptive practices.
