When considering the best way to distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to assess your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general guidelines to consider:

1. **401(k) Plans**:
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match, aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of it, as this is essentially free money.
   - **Contribution Limits**: Be aware of the contribution limits and try to contribute as much as you can, especially if you are close to retirement.

2. **IRAs (Traditional and Roth)**:
   - **Tax Considerations**: If you expect to be in a lower tax bracket during retirement, a Traditional IRA may be more beneficial. Conversely, a Roth IRA is advantageous if you think your tax rate will increase in the future, allowing tax-free withdrawals.
   - **Diversification of Tax Treatments**: Having both a Traditional and a Roth IRA can provide flexibility in managing taxes during retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Liquidity Needs**: If you anticipate needing access to funds before retirement, consider contributing to taxable accounts, as they offer more flexibility without penalties for early withdrawal.
   - **Investment Strategy**: Taxable accounts can be beneficial for investments that generate capital gains rather than ordinary income, like stocks, which may be taxed at a lower rate.

4. **General Strategy**:
   - **Prioritize tax-advantaged accounts**: Max out contributions to your 401(k) and IRA accounts first based on your eligibility and financial situation, then allocate funds to taxable accounts.
   - **Asset Allocation**: Diversify investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) in all accounts to manage risk while considering the tax implications of each account type.

5. **Regular Review**:
   - **Rebalance Portfolio**: Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on market performance, life changes, or shifts in your financial goals.
   - **Consult a Financial Advisor**: Consider seeking professional advice tailored to your specific financial situation.
