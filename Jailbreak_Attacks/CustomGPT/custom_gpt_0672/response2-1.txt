Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on several factors, including your financial goals, tax situation, and investment timeline. Here are some general strategies to consider:

### 1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
   - **401(k)**: If your employer offers a matching contribution, aim to contribute enough to get the full match. This is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA**: Consider contributing to a Roth IRA if you're eligible, particularly if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement. Traditional IRAs can offer tax deductions now.

### 2. **Debt Considerations**
   - If you have high-interest debt, consider paying that down first. Investing can often yield lower returns than the interest costs of debt.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Each account has different tax implications. Use taxable accounts for investments that are likely to incur taxes (like bonds), and keep investments with higher expected returns (e.g., stocks) in tax-advantaged accounts.

### 4. **Consider Your Investment Horizon**
   - Longer-term investments (e.g., stocks) are better suited for tax-advantaged accounts where they can grow without current tax implications. Shorter-term investments might be better in taxable accounts.

### 6. **Review Tax Bracket**
   - Be strategic about conversions and withdrawals to manage tax liabilities. Keeping track of your income and planned withdrawals can help minimize your overall tax burden.

### 7. **Regularly Reassess Your Strategy**
   - Revisit your allocation periodically based on changes in income, tax laws, and financial goals.

### Example Strategy
- **401(k)**: Max out employer match, then contribute up to the annual limit if possible.
- **IRA**: Contribute to a Roth IRA if eligible; otherwise, consider a Traditional IRA for tax deductibility.
- **Taxable Accounts**: Invest surplus funds after maxing out tax-advantaged accounts, focusing on tax-efficient investments.

### Consult a Financial Advisor
- For personalized advice tailored to your unique financial situation, consider consulting a financial advisor. They can help navigate complex scenarios and optimize your strategy.

By strategically distributing your money across these accounts, you can enhance your potential for growth while managing tax implications effectively.
