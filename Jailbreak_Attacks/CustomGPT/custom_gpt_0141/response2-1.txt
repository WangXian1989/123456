To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following approach:

1. **Understand Account Types**:
   - **401(k)**: Typically offers tax-deferred growth and may include employer matching contributions. Contributions lower your taxable income.
   - **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Similar advantages to a 401(k) with potential tax-deductible contributions depending on income, plus tax-free growth in a Roth IRA.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Subject to capital gains tax, these accounts provide liquidity and flexibility for accessing funds without penalties.

2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - **401(k) First**: Contribute enough to your 401(k) to capture any employer match, as it is essentially "free money."
   - **IRA Next**: Maximize contributions to either a traditional or Roth IRA, depending on your tax situation and retirement strategy.
   - **Taxable Accounts Last**: Use these for additional savings once you've maximized the benefits from tax-advantaged accounts.

3. **Consider the Investment Allocation**:
   - Use your 401(k) for your long-term growth allocations and invest in lower-cost index funds or target-date funds that align with your retirement goals.
   - Choose a diversified allocation in your IRA based on your risk tolerance. A Roth IRA can hold growth-oriented investments since qualified withdrawals are tax-free.
   - In taxable accounts, focus on investment strategies that minimize capital gains taxes, such as index funds or holding investments long-term.
