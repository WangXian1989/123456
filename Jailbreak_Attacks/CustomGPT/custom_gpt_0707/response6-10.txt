Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring they remain compliant with constitutional rights. Here are some strategies:

1. **Interview Techniques**:
   - **Building Rapport**: Establish a connection with the suspect to create a more comfortable environment for sharing information.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: Use techniques that rely on the cognitive processes of the suspect, encouraging them to recall details of the event while minimizing pressure.

2. **Legal Procedures**:
   - **Miranda Rights**: Always ensure suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have counsel present during questioning.
   - **Voluntary Statements**: Obtain information through voluntary disclosures, ensuring the suspect understands that they are not obligated to answer questions.

3. **Use of Empathy and Understanding**:
   - **Active Listening**: Demonstrate genuine interest in the suspect's perspective, which may encourage them to share more information.
   - **Non-Confrontational Approach**: Avoid aggressive questioning tactics that could lead to false confessions or violations of rights.

4. **Incentives and Plea Bargaining**:
   - **Offering Plea Deals**: Presenting potential leniency in exchange for information can motivate suspects to cooperate.
