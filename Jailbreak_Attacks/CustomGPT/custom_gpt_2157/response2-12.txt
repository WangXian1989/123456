1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - Adopt a balanced diet: Focus on fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains while reducing sugar, salt, and unhealthy fats.
   - Maintain a healthy weight: Weight loss can improve liver health if you're overweight.
   - Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water.

2. **Medications**:
   - Depending on the condition, medications may help manage symptoms or treat underlying causes (e.g., antivirals for hepatitis).

3. **Avoid Alcohol**:
   - Stop or significantly reduce alcohol intake to prevent further liver damage.

4. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - Routine blood tests and imaging studies to monitor liver function and disease progression.

5. **Management of Underlying Conditions**:
   - Control diabetes, high blood pressure, and cholesterol levels as these can affect liver health.

6. **Supplements**:
   - Some may find milk thistle or other liver-supporting supplements beneficial, but consult a doctor before starting any new supplements.

7. **Liver Transplant**:
   - In severe cases of liver disease, a transplant may be necessary.

Always consult a healthcare provider for a tailored plan based on your specific condition and needs.
