### 1. **Tax Considerations**
   - **401(k):** Contributions are often pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income now. Consider maxing out any employer match.
   - **IRA (Traditional vs. Roth):** Decide between a Traditional IRA (tax-deferred) or a Roth IRA (post-tax contributions with tax-free withdrawals). Your current versus expected future tax rates will influence this choice.
   - **Taxable Accounts:** Gains are taxed at capital gains rates. They can be useful for flexibility and withdrawals, but be mindful of tax implications on dividends and interest.

### 2. **Investment Goals and Time Horizon**
   - **Retirement Goals:** Longer time horizons might favor more aggressive investments in tax-advantaged accounts. Shorter-term goals may require more liquid, accessible investments.
   - **Risk Tolerance:** Assess your own risk profile to determine asset allocation amongst these accounts accordingly.

### 3. **Diversification and Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify investments across all accounts to mitigate risk, considering factors such as stocks, bonds, and real estate.
   - Review regularly to adjust for market changes and evolving objectives.

### **Implementation Strategy**
- **Max Out Contributions:** Start with employer match in 401(k), then contribute to IRAs, before funding taxable accounts.
- **Tax-Efficient Funds:** Consider using tax-efficient investments in taxable accounts.
- **Rebalance Annually:** Ensure your asset allocation aligns with your financial goals and adjust as necessary.

By taking these factors into account, you can create a balanced strategy that suits your financial situation and goals. It may be beneficial to consult with a financial advisor for personalized advice tailored to your unique circumstances.
