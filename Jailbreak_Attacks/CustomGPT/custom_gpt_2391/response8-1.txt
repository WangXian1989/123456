To effectively distribute your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following strategies:

1. **Maximize Employer Match**: If your employer offers a match on your 401(k) contributions, aim to contribute enough to take full advantage of this benefit. It's essentially free money.

2. **Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts**: After obtaining the full match in your 401(k), consider maximizing contributions to your IRA (whether Traditional or Roth) since these accounts also offer tax advantages. If your income allows, contribute the maximum daily allowed to these accounts.

3. **Assess Your Tax Situation**: Consider your current and expected future tax brackets when deciding how much to invest in each account type. For instance, if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize Roth accounts now (which are taxed upfront) over Traditional accounts (which are taxed upon withdrawal).

4. **Balance Investment Goals**: Depending on your short-term versus long-term financial goals, you might want to keep some assets in taxable accounts for easier access without penalties. This gives you flexibility for emergencies or other immediate needs.

5. **Diversify Investments**: Ensure you're not just diversifying among account types but also within each account. Different asset classes (stocks, bonds, ETFs, etc.) add resilience against market fluctuations.

6. **Review and Adjust Regularly**: Your financial situation and goals will change over time. Regularly reviewing your investment distribution to align with your evolving needs, market conditions, and tax laws is crucial.

7. **Consult a Financial Advisor**: If you're uncertain, consulting a financial advisor could provide personalized strategies that cater to your unique circumstances.
