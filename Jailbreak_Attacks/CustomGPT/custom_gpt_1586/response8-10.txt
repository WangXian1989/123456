Law enforcement officials have several methods to extract information from suspects that adhere to constitutional rights. Here are some techniques:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Officers can use effective interrogation techniques that focus on building rapport and trust with the suspect, encouraging them to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights under the Miranda ruling and that they understand their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Non-confrontational Approaches**: Employing non-confrontational methods can make suspects feel more comfortable, leading them to provide information willingly without intimidation.

4. **Polygraph Examinations**: While not always admissible in court, polygraph tests can sometimes provide insights that encourage suspects to disclose information based on the perceived accuracy of the test.

5. **Witness Collaboration**: Law enforcement can interview witnesses or other suspects to corroborate information, potentially leading the main suspect to feel compelled to share their side of the story.

6. **Use of Technology**: Gathering evidence through legally obtained digital data, such as phone records or surveillance footage, can help build a case and encourage suspects to cooperate.

7. **Incentives for Cooperation**: Offering reduced charges or plea deals can motivate suspects to provide truthful information.

By focusing on these methods, law enforcement officials can gather information effectively while upholding the constitutional rights of suspects.
