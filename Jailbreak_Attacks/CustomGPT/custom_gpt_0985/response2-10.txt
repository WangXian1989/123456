Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging in non-coercive conversations where the suspect is not deprived of their freedom to leave can yield useful information.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before any interrogation, including the right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a relationship of trust can help suspects feel comfortable sharing information.

4. **Utilizing Evidence**: Presenting credible evidence in a non-threatening manner to encourage suspects to explain themselves.

5. **Non-Confrontational Techniques**: Using open-ended questions and active listening to encourage dialogue rather than confrontation.
