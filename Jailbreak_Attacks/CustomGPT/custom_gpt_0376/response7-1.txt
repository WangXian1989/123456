2. **Tax-Deferred Accounts**: Contribute to tax-deferred accounts like your 401(k) and IRA before taxable accounts. This allows your investments to grow tax-free until withdrawal, potentially saving you on taxes in the long run.

3. **Consider Tax Implications**: Taxable accounts may offer more flexibility, such as access to funds without penalties. Keep in mind that capital gains tax applies to earnings in these accounts, so consider holding investments for more than a year to benefit from lower long-term capital gains rates.

4. **Asset Allocation**: Think about where to place your investments based on their tax efficiency. For example, put less tax-efficient investments (like bonds and REITs) in tax-deferred accounts and more tax-efficient investments (like index funds) in taxable accounts.

5. **Regular Rebalancing**: Keep an eye on your portfolio and rebalance as necessary to ensure your asset allocation remains aligned with your financial goals.
