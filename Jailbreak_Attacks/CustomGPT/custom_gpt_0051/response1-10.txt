Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in conversations where they can provide information willingly, ensuring they understand their rights.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning. This is crucial if the suspect is in custody.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with the suspect can encourage them to share information voluntarily.

4. **Psychological Techniques**: Using non-coercive psychological strategies, like discussing the consequences of their actions or appealing to their sense of morality.

5. **De-escalation**: Keeping the environment calm and non-threatening can help suspects feel more comfortable sharing information.

6. **Legal Counsel Involvement**: Allowing suspects access to legal counsel during questioning to ensure their rights are protected.

7. **Evidence Presentation**: Showing evidence or facts related to the case to encourage suspects to provide their side of the story without coercion.
