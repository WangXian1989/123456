Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Interviewing Techniques**: Using rapport-building and open-ended questions to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are aware of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Voluntary Statements**: Encouraging suspects to provide information without coercion, ensuring they understand they are free to leave.

4. **Polygraph Testing**: Using lie detector tests, if the suspect consents, can help assess credibility.

5. **Informal Conversations**: Engaging suspects in casual talk where they might volunteer information without realizing they are being interrogated.
