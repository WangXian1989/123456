Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights. Here are a few key techniques:

1. **Interviewing**: Conducting interviews in a non-coercive manner, utilizing open-ended questions to encourage dialogue.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing trust and a good relationship with the suspect can lead to more cooperative interactions.

4. **Using Evidence**: Presenting compelling evidence in a way that encourages suspects to provide information voluntarily.

5. **Polygraph Tests**: While not always admissible in court, they can be used to gauge truthfulness and elicit confessions.

6. **Emphasizing Consequences**: Making suspects aware of the potential outcomes of their situation can sometimes lead them to confess.
