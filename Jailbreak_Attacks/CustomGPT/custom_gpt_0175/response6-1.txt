### 1. Prioritize Tax-Advantaged Accounts
   - **Maximize Employer Matches**: Contribute at least enough to your 401(k) to take full advantage of any employer matching contributions. This is essentially "free money."
   - **Contribute to IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth or traditional IRA, depending on your tax situation. Roth IRAs provide tax-free growth, whereas traditional IRAs offer tax-deferred growth.

### 2. Allocation Strategy
   - **401(k)**: Focus on higher growth investments within your 401(k), as the growth is tax-deferred until withdrawal. Consider a mix of stock mutual funds or ETFs.
   - **IRA**: Balance risk based on your age and retirement timeline. A younger individual may emphasize stocks, while someone closer to retirement might allocate more toward bonds.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that generate qualified dividends or long-term capital gains, which are taxed at lower rates. Consider tax-loss harvesting strategies to offset gains.

### 3. Ongoing Adjustments
   - **Periodic Reviews**: Regularly assess the performance of your investments and rebalance as necessary to maintain your desired asset allocation.
   - **Tax Implications**: Be mindful of the tax implications of your withdrawals and distributions, especially from taxable accounts.

### Conclusion
- **Continuous Education**: Stay informed about changes in tax laws and financial strategies to optimize your distribution methods.
- **Professional Advice**: Consider consulting a financial advisor for personalized strategies that align with your financial goals.

By strategically distributing your investments among these accounts, you can enhance growth potential while managing your tax obligations effectively.
