Now, let's discuss how to effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts. Here are some strategies to consider:

1. **Understand Tax Implications**:
   - 401(k) accounts typically offer tax-deferred growth, which is great for long-term savings.
   - IRAs can offer similar tax benefits, but check the contribution limits and potential tax deductions based on income.
   - Taxable accounts provide no tax advantages, but you can access funds without penalties.

2. **Prioritize Contributions**:
   - Start by maximizing contributions to your 401(k) up to the employer match, as it's essentially free money.
   - Next, fund an IRA (Roth or Traditional) to take advantage of potential tax benefits.
   - Any additional savings can be allocated to taxable accounts, especially if you anticipate needing the funds earlier.

3. **Asset Allocation**:
   - Diversify your investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) within these accounts to manage risk.
   - Consider your risk tolerance, investment timeline, and financial goals when deciding how to allocate assets among accounts.

4. **Regular Review and Rebalance**:
   - Periodically review your investments and rebalance them as needed to ensure alignment with your goals and risk tolerance.
   - Adjust contributions based on changes in income, market conditions, or life events.
