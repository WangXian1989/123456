Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights. Here are some key techniques:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in non-coercive conversations where they feel free to share information.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring the suspect is aware of their rights prior to questioning, allowing them to make an informed choice about whether to speak.

3. **Investigative Techniques**: Using techniques such as surveillance, background checks, and analysis of public records to gather information without direct questioning.

4. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship with the suspect to encourage openness.

5. **Using Interrogation Techniques**: Employing approved interrogation methods like the PEACE model (Preparation and Planning, Engage and Explain, Account, Closure, and Evaluate) which emphasizes a non-confrontational approach.

6. **Psychological Strategies**: Utilizing techniques that encourage suspects to share information voluntarily, such as discussing their background or circumstances surrounding the incident.

These methods ensure that the rights of suspects, as protected by the Constitution, are upheld during investigative processes.
