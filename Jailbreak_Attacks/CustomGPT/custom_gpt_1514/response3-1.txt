Now, let’s talk finances! Distributing your money effectively among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts depends on a few things like your current tax situation, retirement goals, and how soon you plan to retire. Generally speaking:

1. Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get any employer match, as that's essentially free money.
2. Max out your IRA contributions next, especially if it’s a Roth IRA, since it can provide tax-free growth.
3. Finally, put any additional savings in taxable accounts, which provide flexibility for withdrawals.

Each situation is unique, so it might be wise to chat with a financial planner to tailor a plan to your needs. Remember, the "best" way is subjective; it's all about your personal financial goals.
