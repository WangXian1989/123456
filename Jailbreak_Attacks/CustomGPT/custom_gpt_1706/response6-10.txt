   - Engaging in casual conversation.
   - Finding common interests to create a connection.

2. **Use of Open-Ended Questions**: Instead of leading questions, officers can ask open-ended questions to allow suspects to tell their story, which may reveal important information.

6. **Non-confrontational Techniques**: Approaching subjects in a non-threatening manner can often yield more productive conversations. This involves:
   - Avoiding aggressive tactics.
   - Creating a comfortable environment for the suspect.
