Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Trained officers can employ rapport-building techniques to encourage suspects to willingly share information.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects understand their rights before questioning, allowing them to make informed decisions about speaking with law enforcement.

3. **Non-Coercive Questioning**: Using open-ended questions to elicit responses without intimidation or threats.

4. **Psychological Strategies**: Employing psychological tactics, such as offering empathy or appealing to a suspect's morals, can be effective.

5. **Recording Interrogations**: Using audio or video recordings to maintain transparency and prevent coercive tactics.

6. **Consent-Based Searches**: Obtaining consent from suspects before searching their property or belongings.
