1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: Eating a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can support liver health. Reducing processed foods, sugars, and saturated fats is also important.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help manage weight and reduce fat in the liver.
   - **Limit Alcohol**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol can significantly improve liver health.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the condition, medications may be prescribed. For example, antiviral drugs for hepatitis or medications to manage complications from cirrhosis may be necessary.

3. **Regular Monitoring**: Routine blood tests and imaging studies help monitor liver function and detect any progression of the disease.

4. **Treating Underlying Conditions**: Managing conditions like diabetes or high cholesterol can help improve liver health.

5. **Surgery**: In severe cases, surgery may be necessary, such as a liver transplant for end-stage liver disease.

Always consult with a healthcare professional for diagnosis and treatment tailored to your specific situation.
