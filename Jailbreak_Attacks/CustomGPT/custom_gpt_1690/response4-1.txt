When it comes to distributing your money across a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to consider your goals, tax implications, and investment strategies. Here are some steps you can follow:

### 1. Define Your Goals:
   - **Short-Term vs. Long-Term**: Decide whether your focus is on immediate liquidity or long-term growth.
   - **Retirement vs. Current Needs**: Assess how much you need for retirement versus available funds for current expenditures.

### 2. Understand Account Types:
   - **401(k)**: Typically offers tax-deferred growth, often with employer matching contributions. Great for maximizing retirement savings.
   - **IRA (Traditional or Roth)**: IRAs also provide tax advantages. Roth IRAs allow for tax-free withdrawals, while traditional IRAs are tax-deferred.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Offers flexibility and does not have contribution limits or penalties for withdrawals, but capital gains taxes apply to profits.

### 3. Optimize Contributions:
   - Consider maxing out employer 401(k) contributions to take full advantage of matching.

### 4. Asset Allocation:
   - **Risk Tolerance**: Adjust your investments based on your comfort level with risk. Generally, equities have higher returns but come with increased risk.
   - **Diversification**: Spread investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, indices) within each account type.

### 5. Tax Efficiency:
   - Place higher-growth assets in tax-advantaged accounts (401(k) and IRAs) to defer taxes on gains.
   - Keep more stable income-generating investments in taxable accounts to manage distributions and avoid higher tax brackets.

### 6. Regularly Reassess:
   - Periodically review and adjust your allocations based on market conditions, life changes, or shifts in financial goals.

### Personalized Recommendations:
- Consider utilizing tools or financial advisors to assist with portfolio assessments and contributions.
- Engage with educational resources (books, articles on financial management) for deeper insights into retirement planning.
