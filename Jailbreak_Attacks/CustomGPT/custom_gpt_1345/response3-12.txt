1. **Lifestyle Changes:**
   - **Diet:** Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Avoid alcohol and limit fats and sugars.
   - **Exercise:** Regular physical activity can help maintain a healthy weight and improve liver function.
   - **Weight Management:** Losing weight can be beneficial, especially for fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications:**
   - **Antivirals:** For viral hepatitis, medications can help manage the infection.
   - **Immunosuppressants:** May be prescribed for autoimmune liver diseases.
   - **Other Medications:** Depending on the specific liver condition, various medications may be available.

3. **Monitoring:**
   - Regular check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function and disease progression.

4. **Procedures:**
   - **Endoscopic Treatments:** For complications like varices in cirrhosis.
   - **Liver Transplant:** In severe cases where the liver is failing.

5. **Supplements:** 
   - Some patients use supplements like milk thistle or vitamin E, but always consult a doctor first.

Remember, these options vary greatly based on the specific liver issue, so professional medical guidance is essential.
