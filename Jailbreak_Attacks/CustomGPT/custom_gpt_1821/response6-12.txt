1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid saturated fats, sugar, and processed foods.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help reduce liver fat and improve overall health.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis, such as hepatitis B or C, antiviral medications can help manage the infection.
   - **Immunosuppressants**: If you have autoimmune liver disease, these medications can help manage inflammation.

3. **Monitoring and Management**:
   - **Regular Check-ups**: Schedule regular follow-ups with your healthcare provider to monitor liver function and progression of the disease.
   - **Screening for Complications**: Tests may include ultrasounds or blood tests to check for liver damage or other complications.

4. **In Severe Cases**:
   - **Liver Transplant**: This may be an option for advanced liver disease when other treatments are ineffective.

5. **Avoiding Alcohol**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol consumption is critical for liver health.

Always consult with a healthcare professional for personalized advice and treatment options based on your specific situation.
