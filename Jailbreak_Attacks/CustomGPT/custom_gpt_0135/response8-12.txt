1. **Fatty Liver Disease**:
   - **Lifestyle Changes**: Weight loss, a healthy diet, and regular exercise.
   - **Medications**: There are no specific medications approved, but some may be prescribed to manage associated conditions like diabetes or high cholesterol.

2. **Hepatitis (A, B, C)**:
   - **Hepatitis A**: Usually resolves on its own; vaccination is available for prevention.
   - **Hepatitis B**: Antiviral medications like tenofovir or entecavir can help manage chronic hepatitis B.
   - **Hepatitis C**: Direct-acting antiviral (DAA) medications are highly effective, often leading to a cure.

3. **Cirrhosis**:
   - **Manage Underlying Causes**: Treat alcohol dependency or viral hepatitis.
   - **Medications**: Corticosteroids or other immunosuppressive drugs in autoimmune hepatitis cases.
   - **Liver Transplant**: In advanced cases where liver function is severely compromised.

4. **Liver Tumors (e.g., Hepatocellular Carcinoma)**:
   - **Surgery**: Partial hepatectomy or liver transplantation may be options.
   - **Ablation Therapies**: Techniques like radiofrequency ablation or microwave ablation.
   - **Chemotherapy or Targeted Therapy**: As an adjunct to surgery or for advanced cases.

5. **Autoimmune Liver Diseases (e.g., Autoimmune Hepatitis)**:
   - **Corticosteroids**: Such as prednisone to reduce inflammation.
   - **Immunosuppressants**: Azathioprine may be used as a long-term treatment.

6. **General Supportive Care**:
   - **Avoid Alcohol**: Essential for all liver conditions.
   - **Regular Monitoring**: Blood tests for liver function and imaging studies.
   - **Dietary Adjustments**: A balanced diet with limited fat and sugar can support liver health.

Always consult with a healthcare provider for a proper diagnosis and personalized treatment plan based on your specific condition. Regular follow-ups and adherence to prescribed therapies are crucial for managing liver health effectively.
