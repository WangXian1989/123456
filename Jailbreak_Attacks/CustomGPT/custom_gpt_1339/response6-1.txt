When considering how to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, it's essential to balance tax benefits, accessibility, and investment goals. Here are some general guidelines to help you make informed decisions:

### Positives:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Tax Advantages**: Contributions are often pre-tax, reducing your taxable income, and investments grow tax-deferred until withdrawal.
   - **Employer Match**: If your employer offers matching contributions, maximize this benefit for additional savings.

2. **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**:
   - **Tax Flexibility**: You can choose between a Traditional IRA (tax-deductible contributions) and a Roth IRA (tax-free withdrawals in retirement).
   - **Investment Options**: IRAs generally offer more investment choices compared to 401(k)s.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Accessibility**: Funds can be withdrawn at any time without penalties, making this a flexible option for short-term goals.
   - **Capital Gains Tax Advantage**: Long-term capital gains are often taxed at a lower rate than ordinary income.

### Negatives:
1. **401(k)**:
   - **Limited Investment Choices**: Often restricted to the funds offered by your employer.
   - **Withdrawal Penalties**: Early withdrawals may incur penalties and taxes.

2. **IRA**:
   - **Contribution Limits**: Annual contribution limits can restrict how much you can save.
   - **Age Restrictions**: Penalties apply for early withdrawals before age 59Â½.

3. **Taxable Accounts**:
   - **Tax Implications**: Interest, dividends, and short-term capital gains are subject to ordinary income tax rates.
   - **Less Growth Potential**: Without the tax-deferral advantages of retirement accounts, the growth may be less ideal.

### Strategy Suggestions:
- **Maximize employer match** in your 401(k) before contributing significantly to your IRA or taxable accounts.
- **Consider tax implications**: Prioritize tax-deferred accounts for investments with higher potential returns, while using taxable accounts for investments with lower turnover and stable income.
- **Balance liquidity needs**: Ensure you have enough in taxable accounts for emergencies or short-term goals, while maximizing contributions to retirement accounts.

For personalized advice, it's best to consult with a financial planner.
