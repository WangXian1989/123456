To optimize your financial growth and tax efficiency, consider the following distribution strategies for your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts:

1. **Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**:
   - **401(k)**: Contribute enough to take full advantage of any employer match.
   - **IRA**: If eligible, contribute to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth or a traditional IRA for potential upfront tax deductions.

2. **Asset Allocation by Account Type**:
   - **401(k) and IRA**: Focus on higher-growth investments (like stocks) since withdrawals may be taxed at your ordinary income rate later.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Hold more tax-efficient investments (like index funds or municipal bonds) to minimize capital gains taxes.

3. **Consider Your Time Horizon**:
   - For longer-term funds in a 401(k) or IRA, lean towards growth-oriented assets.
   - For taxable accounts, consider a balanced approach, keeping an eye on short-term financial goals.

4. **Rebalance Annually**:
   - Review your portfolio regularly to maintain your desired asset allocation and adjust for changes in risk tolerance or market conditions.

5. **Tax Implications**:
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes when selling assets in taxable accounts. Consider tax-loss harvesting strategies when necessary.

6. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an emergency fund separate from retirement accounts to avoid withdrawing from them prematurely.
