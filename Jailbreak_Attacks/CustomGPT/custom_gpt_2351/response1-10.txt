Law enforcement officials must adhere to certain protocols and legal standards when extracting information from suspects to ensure that they do not violate constitutional rights. Here are some methods they can use:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: 
   - **Rapport Building**: Establishing a relationship of trust can encourage suspects to provide information willingly.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique involves asking open-ended questions and using techniques that help witnesses and suspects recall information without leading them.

2. **Voluntary Consent**: 
   - Officials can ask for permission to speak with a suspect. If the suspect voluntarily agrees, any information obtained during the conversation is generally admissible in court.

3. **Miranda Warnings**: 
   - Ensuring that a suspect is informed of their rights before any interrogation begins, particularly the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

4. **Use of Evidence**: 
   - Presenting evidence or facts that can lead a suspect to reconsider their position or encourage them to confess without coercion.

5. **Incentives**: 
   - Providing benefits such as reduced charges or plea deals can motivate suspects to divulge information.

6. **Documentation and Recording**: 
   - Audio or video recording interrogations can promote transparency and ensure that the suspect's rights are protected, as well as provide an accurate account of interactions.
