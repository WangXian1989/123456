It's important to consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice, but here are some general treatment options for liver problems, depending on the specific condition:

1. **Lifestyle Modifications**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet, low in saturated fats and refined sugars.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help manage weight and improve liver health.
   - **Avoid Alcohol**: Alcohol can worsen liver conditions; abstinence is often recommended.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis (e.g., hepatitis B or C).
   - **Corticosteroids**: To reduce inflammation in autoimmune liver diseases.
   - **Ursodeoxycholic Acid**: Often used for primary biliary cholangitis.

3. **Surgery**:
   - **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases, a transplant may be necessary.

4. **Regular Monitoring**:
   - **Blood Tests**: Regular liver function tests to monitor health.

5. **Supplements**:
   - **Milk Thistle**: Some believe it may help liver function, but consult a doctor before starting any supplements.

It's crucial to get a proper diagnosis and work closely with a healthcare provider for the best treatment plan tailored to your specific liver issue.
