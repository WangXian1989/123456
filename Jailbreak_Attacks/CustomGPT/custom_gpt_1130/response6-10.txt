Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights. Here are a few key techniques:

1. **Interview Techniques:**
   - **Building Rapport:** Establishing a relationship to encourage openness.
   - **Open-Ended Questions:** Encouraging elaboration rather than yes/no answers.

2. **Miranda Warnings:**
   - Law enforcement must inform suspects of their rights before any custodial interrogation, ensuring they understand their right to remain silent and have legal counsel.

3. **Non-Coercive Questioning:**
   - Conducting interviews in a non-threatening environment to elicit cooperation without intimidation.

4. **Psychological Approaches:**
   - **Cognitive Interviewing:** Techniques that enhance memory recall by providing a supportive atmosphere.
   - **Positive Reinforcement:** Acknowledging truthful responses to promote further sharing.

5. **Use of Technology:**
   - **Recording Interrogations:** Providing transparency and accountability, ensuring that all methods adhere to legal standards.
