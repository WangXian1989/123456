### 1. Lifestyle Changes
- **Diet**: Adopt a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Avoid alcohol and processed foods.
- **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help reduce fat in the liver and improve overall liver function.

### 2. Medication
- **Antivirals**: For viral hepatitis, medications like interferons and antivirals (e.g., tenofovir) may be prescribed.
- **Corticosteroids**: These can help reduce inflammation in autoimmune liver diseases.

### 3. Monitoring and Management
- **Regular Check-ups**: Routine blood tests and imaging can track liver health and function.
- **Management of Underlying Conditions**: Controlling diabetes, cholesterol levels, and blood pressure can significantly aid liver health.

### 4. Surgical Options
- **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases (e.g., end-stage liver disease), a transplant may be necessary.
