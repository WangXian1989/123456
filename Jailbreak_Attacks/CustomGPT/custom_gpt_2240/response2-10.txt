Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights, including:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**:
   - **Rapport Building**: Establishing a connection to encourage openness.
   - **Cognitive Interviewing**: Asking open-ended questions to enhance recall and gather details without pressure.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Before interrogation, ensuring suspects are informed of their rights to silence and an attorney.

3. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to share information willingly without coercion.

4. **Non-Verbal Cues**: Observing body language and expressions to gauge truthfulness without aggressive questioning.

5. **Psychological Tactics**: Using strategic questioning that encourages suspects to reflect on their actions without intimidation.

6. **Witness Collaboration**: Offering the suspect an opportunity to provide evidence supporting their perspective, fostering a more collaborative atmosphere.

7. **Transparency**: Clearly explaining the investigation process and consequences to demystify the procedures involved.
