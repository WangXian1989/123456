2. **Miranda Rights**: Officers must inform suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning, ensuring they understand these rights before proceeding.

3. **Voluntary Statements**: Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily without coercion or threats, ensuring that any statement made can be used in court.

4. **Interviewing Techniques**: Employing strategic interviewing techniques that focus on understanding the suspect's perspective rather than aggressive confrontation.

5. **Limited Use of Deception**: Occasionally, law enforcement may use certain forms of deception or misleading information about evidence, as long as it does not cross the line into coercion or threats.

6. **Presence of Counsel**: Allowing suspects to have legal representation during questioning if they request it, ensuring their rights are protected.
