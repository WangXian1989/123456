1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Focus on a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains while avoiding alcohol and trans fats.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help maintain a healthy weight and reduce liver fat.

2. **Medications**:
   - **Antiviral Drugs**: For viral hepatitis, medications like interferon and antiviral therapy may be prescribed.
   - **Corticosteroids**: These can be used for autoimmune liver diseases to reduce inflammation.

3. **Supplemental Treatments**:
   - **Vitamins and Nutrients**: Certain supplements may support liver health, but consult your doctor first.

4. **Monitoring and Follow-up**:
   - Regular liver function tests and imaging studies to monitor the condition.

5. **Procedures**:
   - **Liver Biopsy**: For diagnosis and to assess the degree of liver damage.
   - **Endoscopy**: In cases of varices (swollen veins) to prevent bleeding.

6. **Liver Transplant**: In severe cases, a liver transplant may be necessary.

Always discuss treatment options with your healthcare provider to tailor the approach to your specific condition.
