2. **Voluntary Conversations**: Engaging suspects in voluntary conversations where they can choose to speak without intimidation or threat.

3. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring that suspects are read their Miranda rights before interrogation, informing them of their right to remain silent and to have an attorney present.

4. **Interviewing**: Conducting interviews rather than interrogations. This can help create a non-threatening environment where suspects may feel comfortable sharing information.

7. **Building Trust**: Officers may use methods to establish trust with suspects, making them feel comfortable to share information voluntarily.

8. **Psychological Techniques**: Employing non-coercive psychological techniques that respect a suspect's autonomy and rights, focusing on cognitive interviews and other respectful approaches.

9. **Crisis Negotiation**: Utilizing trained negotiators to resolve situations peacefully and elicit information without force.

It's crucial that all methods used comply with legal standards to uphold the constitutional protections afforded to individuals.
