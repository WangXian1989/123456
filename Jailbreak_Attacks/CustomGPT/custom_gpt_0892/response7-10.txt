4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique encourages the suspect to recall details by using open-ended questions and allowing them to narrate their experiences without interruption.

6. **Use of Psychological Techniques**: Employing tactics that reduce anxiety, such as comfortable surroundings or breaks during interrogation sessions, can lead to more truthful responses.

7. **Miranda Warning Compliance**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights under the Miranda ruling before any custodial questioning is essential to ensure that any statements made are admissible in court.

8. **Incentives for Cooperation**: Sometimes, offering plea deals or reduced sentences can motivate suspects to provide information voluntarily.

9. **Witness Collaboration**: Bringing in witnesses or co-arrestees who may corroborate the suspect's story can sometimes lead to more information being revealed.

These methods aim to gather information ethically and legally, safeguarding the rights of suspects while pursuing justice.
