α The best way to distribute your money among your 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts typically depends on your financial goals, investment time horizon, and tax situation. Here are some general guidelines:

1. **401(k) Accounts**: Maximize your employer's match, as this is essentially free money. Contribute enough to receive full matching if available. 

2. **IRA Contributions**: If you qualify, consider contributing to a Roth IRA for tax-free growth, or a traditional IRA for tax-deductible contributions. This depends on your income level and whether you anticipate being in a higher tax bracket during retirement.

3. **Taxable Accounts**: Use these for investments that may not fit well in tax-advantaged accounts, such as individual stocks. Additionally, consider asset location strategies, where you place high-growth investments in tax-deferred accounts (like a 401(k) or traditional IRA) and income-generating investments (like bonds) in taxable accounts to minimize tax liabilities.

Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor a strategy that fits your personal situation.
