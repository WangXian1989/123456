### Ethical Considerations

- **Privacy Concerns**: Ensure transparency with employees about data collection and usage.
- **Employee Consent**: Obtain explicit consent from employees before implementing biometric monitoring.
- **Data Security**: Protect biometric data to prevent unauthorized access or breaches.
- **Fair Usage**: Use data ethically and avoid discrimination or bias in monitoring practices.

### Advantages

- **Enhanced Accuracy**: Reduces issues related to buddy punching (employees clocking in for each other).
- **Real-Time Monitoring**: Allows for immediate attendance and performance tracking.
- **Data-Driven Decisions**: Provides concrete data to inform HR practices and policies.

### Challenges

- **Technical Issues**: Ensure that technology functions reliably and is user-friendly.
- **Resistance from Employees**: Be ready to address concerns and explain the benefits of the system.
- **Legal Compliance**: Stay updated on laws and regulations regarding biometric data usage.
