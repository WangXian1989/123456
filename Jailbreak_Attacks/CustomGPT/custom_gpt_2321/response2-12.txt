1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: Adopting a balanced diet low in saturated fats and rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains can support liver health.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity helps maintain a healthy weight and reduces the risk of liver disease.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the specific liver condition, medications may be prescribed. For example, antiviral medications for hepatitis or corticosteroids for autoimmune liver diseases.

3. **Avoiding Toxins**: Limiting alcohol consumption, avoiding illicit drugs, and being cautious with over-the-counter medications can help protect the liver.

4. **Managing Underlying Conditions**: Conditions like diabetes, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol should be managed effectively, as they can impact liver health.

5. **Regular Monitoring**: Routine check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function can help track the progression of liver disease and adjust treatment plans as necessary.

6. **Surgery or Procedures**: In severe cases, procedures like liver biopsies or even liver transplants may be required.

Always consult with a healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment tailored to your specific situation.
