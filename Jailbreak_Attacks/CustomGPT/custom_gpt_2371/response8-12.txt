I'm not a doctor, but there are several treatment options that you might consider discussing with a healthcare professional if you're experiencing liver problems. Here are some common approaches:

1. **Lifestyle Changes**: 
   - **Diet**: Eating a balanced diet that is low in salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats. Foods rich in antioxidants, such as fruits and vegetables, can also be beneficial.
   - **Exercise**: Regular physical activity can help improve liver health.
   - **Weight Management**: If overweight or obese, losing weight can reduce the risk of fatty liver disease.

2. **Medications**: Depending on the type of liver disease, there are various medications available. For instance:
   - **Antivirals** for viral hepatitis.
   - **Corticosteroids** may be prescribed for conditions like autoimmune hepatitis.

3. **Supplements and Herbal Remedies**: Some people find supplements like milk thistle helpful, but it's important to consult with a healthcare professional first, as some supplements may have side effects or interact with medications.

4. **Regular Monitoring**: For chronic liver conditions, regular check-ups and liver function tests are crucial to monitor the progression of the disease.

5. **Surgery**: In severe cases, procedures such as a liver transplant may be necessary. This is generally considered when liver function is critically low.

6. **Avoiding Alcohol and Toxins**: Reducing or eliminating alcohol consumption and avoiding exposure to drugs and chemicals that can harm the liver.
