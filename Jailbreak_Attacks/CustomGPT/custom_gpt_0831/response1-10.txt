Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring compliance with constitutional rights. Some of these methods include:

1. **Miranda Rights**: Informing suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning can help protect their rights.

2. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews without coercion, pressure, or threats can lead to more reliable information while respecting the suspect's rights.

3. **Building Rapport**: Officers may establish a positive relationship with the suspect, making them feel comfortable to share information voluntarily.

4. **Use of Open-ended Questions**: Asking open-ended questions encourages suspects to provide detailed information without feeling cornered.

5. **Offering Incentives**: In some cases, officers might offer plea deals or other benefits in exchange for truthful information, while ensuring that suspects understand their rights.

6. **De-escalation Techniques**: Using calm and non-aggressive communication can prevent a hostile environment, which encourages cooperation from suspects.

7. **Transparency**: Clearly explaining the process and what is being asked can help suspects feel more at ease, leading to potentially more cooperation.
