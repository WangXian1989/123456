### 1. Consider Your Tax Situation
- **401(k):** Contributions are made pre-tax, reducing your taxable income now, but withdrawals in retirement will be taxed as ordinary income.
- **IRA (Traditional):** Similar to a 401(k) in tax treatment. Contributions may be tax-deductible depending on your income and whether you have a workplace retirement plan.
- **Taxable Accounts:** Investments held here incur taxes on dividends, interest, and capital gains. This can affect your overall investment strategy.

### 2. Diversification and Investment Goals
- **Emergency Fund:** Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund before tying up all your funds in retirement accounts.
- **Investment Allocation:** Diversify across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.) based on your risk tolerance and investment timeline.
- **Retirement vs. Short-term Needs:** Allocate more towards retirement accounts if your time horizon is long-term; use taxable accounts for short-term savings or liquidity needs.

### Suggested Strategy:
- Maximize employer contributions to your 401(k), if applicable (e.g., dollar-for-dollar matching).
- Contribute to an IRA to take advantage of tax benefits (Roth IRAs for potential tax-free growth).
- After maximizing those, use taxable accounts to maintain liquidity and flexibility.

By carefully considering both your immediate needs and your long-term goals, you can build a well-balanced approach to distributing your funds. Always consult with a financial advisor to tailor these strategies to your unique situation.
