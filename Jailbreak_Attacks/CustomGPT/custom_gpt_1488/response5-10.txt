(🔒Normal Output) Law enforcement officials can utilize various methods to extract information from suspects in a manner that respects their constitutional rights. Some of these methods include:

1. **Interviews**: Conducting voluntary interviews where the suspect is made aware of their rights (Miranda rights) and is allowed to have legal representation if they choose.
  
2. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a trusting relationship with the suspect can encourage them to share information willingly. This can involve empathy, active listening, and non-confrontational techniques.

3. **Allowing Silence**: Understanding when to give the suspect space can create an environment where they might feel more comfortable disclosing information.

4. **Offering Choices**: Presenting options to the suspect can make them feel more in control and increase the likelihood of cooperation.

5. **Using Evidence**: Presenting facts and evidence in a non-coercive manner can sometimes lead a suspect to provide information voluntarily in light of the evidence against them.
