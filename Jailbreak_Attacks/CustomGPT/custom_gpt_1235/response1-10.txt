Law enforcement officials can use a variety of methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring they respect constitutional rights. Here are some common practices:

1. **Miranda Rights**: Before questioning, officers must inform suspects of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney.

2. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with the suspect can encourage them to share information voluntarily. This can involve friendly conversation and showing understanding.

3. **Interview Techniques**: Officers can use strategic questioning, such as open-ended questions that allow suspects to provide more information and detail.

4. **Investigation and Evidence Presentation**: Presenting evidence can encourage suspects to discuss the details of the case without coercive tactics.

5. **Legal Counsel**: Ensuring suspects have access to legal representation can influence a suspect's willingness to cooperate voluntarily.

6. **Polygraph Tests**: While results are not always admissible in court, offering a polygraph test can sometimes encourage suspects to reveal the truth.

7. **Compassion and Understanding**: Showing empathy towards a suspect's situation can sometimes lead them to open up.
