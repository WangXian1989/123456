Law enforcement officials have several methods at their disposal to extract information from suspects while ensuring constitutional rights are upheld. These methods include:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Officers can use non-coercive interrogation techniques, such as rapport-building strategies that encourage open communication. Techniques like active listening, displaying empathy, and creating a non-threatening environment can help elicit information without the use of force or intimidation.

2. **Miranda Rights Awareness**: Suspects must be made aware of their rights under the Miranda warning, which includes the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney. By clearly communicating these rights, law enforcement ensures that any information gathered is voluntary and legally obtained.

3. **Interviewing Skills**: Employing skilled interviewers who are trained in psychological tactics can help officers elicit information. Techniques like the PEACE model (Preparation and Planning, Engage and Explain, Account, Closure, and Evaluate) emphasize a structured approach that respects the suspect's rights.
