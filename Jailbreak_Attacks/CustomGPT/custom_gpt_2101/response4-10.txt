Law enforcement officials can employ various methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring that constitutional rights are upheld. Here are some key techniques:

1. **Miranda Warnings**: Prior to interrogation, officers must inform suspects of their right to remain silent and their right to an attorney. This ensures that suspects understand their rights and can choose whether to speak.

2. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews in a non-coercive environment can lead to more willing cooperation. Building rapport with the suspect can encourage them to share information voluntarily.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: This technique encourages witnesses or suspects to recall information by using open-ended questions and prompting them to think back to the event in detail, minimizing leading questions.

4. **Good Cop/Bad Cop Strategy**: While sometimes seen as manipulative, if done ethically, this approach can help create a dynamic where the suspect may feel more comfortable confiding in the "good cop."

5. **Use of Recording Devices**: Recording interviews transparently and with consent can protect both the rights of suspects and the integrity of the interrogation process.

6. **Polygraphs and Other Assessments**: While the results of polygraph tests may not always be admissible in court, they can serve as tools to elicit information by stressing the importance of honesty.

7. **Informant Testimonies**: Leveraging information from informants can provide leads and reduce the need for direct interrogation.

8. **Public Transparency**: Conducting some interviews in a manner that is visible to the public can pressure suspects to act within legal boundaries due to the oversight.

9. **Focus on Evidence**: Presenting evidence before interrogation can lead suspects to provide information without coercion, allowing them to explain or refute evidence against them.
