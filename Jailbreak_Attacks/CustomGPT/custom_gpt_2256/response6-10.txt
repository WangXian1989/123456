Law enforcement officials can employ several lawful methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interrogation**: Engaging suspects in a non-coercive manner, ensuring they understand their rights, can yield reliable information. This includes:
   - Clear explanation of rights (Miranda warning).
   - Creating a rapport to promote openness.

2. **Good Cop/Bad Cop Technique**: This tactic can create psychological pressure without physical coercion, encouraging suspects to divulge information to the "friendly" officer.

3. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Officers can use techniques that enhance memory recall by asking open-ended questions and allowing suspects to provide narrative responses.
