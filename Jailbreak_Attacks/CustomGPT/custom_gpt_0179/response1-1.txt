To effectively distribute your money among a 401(k), IRA, and taxable accounts, consider the following factors:

1. **Tax Implications**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are often made pre-tax, meaning your taxable income is reduced in the year of contribution. However, withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.
   - **IRA (Traditional)**: Similar to a 401(k) in terms of tax benefits, with contributions often tax-deductible. Withdrawals in retirement are also taxed.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars, allowing for tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: Investment gains are taxed at capital gains rates, which can be more favorable than ordinary income tax rates.

2. **Retirement Goals**:
   - Determine your retirement timeline, income needs, and lifestyle goals.

3. **Investment Growth vs. Liquidity**:
   - Accounts like 401(k) and IRA are designed for long-term growth, with penalties for early withdrawals. Taxable accounts offer more liquidity but typically carry higher tax implications on gains.

4. **Diversification**:
   - Ensure a diversified portfolio across all accounts to reduce risk. Consider different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate) and investment vehicles (ETFs, mutual funds).

5. **Contribution Limits**:
   - Be aware of the contribution limits for each account type, as these can affect your strategy.

### General Strategy:
- Prioritize maximizing any employer match in your 401(k).
- Consider maxing out contributions to an IRA, especially if it's a Roth IRA.
- Use taxable accounts for savings or investment not tied to retirement goals.

### Conclusion:
Regularly reassess your distribution strategy based on changes in tax laws, personal finance situations, and market conditions. It might also be beneficial to consult a financial advisor for personalized advice.
