Law enforcement officials can utilize several methods to extract information from suspects while ensuring they do not violate constitutional rights. These methods include:

1. **Interrogation Techniques**: Utilizing rapport-building techniques to encourage suspects to share information voluntarily, while avoiding coercive tactics.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Informing suspects of their rights to remain silent and to have an attorney present during questioning, ensuring any statements made are voluntary and admissible in court.

3. **Witness Interviews**: Conducting interviews with witnesses or associates of the suspect to gather information that may lead to a confession or more details about the case.

4. **Voluntary Statements**: Encouraging suspects to provide statements voluntarily, without any implied threats or promises, thereby respecting their rights.

5. **Polygraph Tests**: Using lie detector tests with the suspect's consent can sometimes encourage them to share truthful information if they believe they will pass the test.

6. **Search Warrants**: Obtaining search warrants when necessary to gather evidence legally, which can provide context and corroborate suspect statements.
