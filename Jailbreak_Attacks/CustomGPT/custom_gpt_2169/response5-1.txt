### 1. **Tax-Advantaged Accounts**
   - **Maximize Contributions**: Start by maxing out your employer's 401(k) match, as this is essentially free money. After that, consider contributing to an IRA (Traditional or Roth), depending on your income level and tax situation.
   - **Traditional vs. Roth IRA**: If you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement, prioritize a Roth IRA for tax-free withdrawals. If you expect to be in a lower bracket, a Traditional IRA may be more beneficial now, as contributions are tax-deductible.

### 2. **Taxable Accounts**
   - After maximizing your 401(k) and IRA contributions, direct excess funds into a taxable account. This allows for greater flexibility with withdrawals and can be strategic for short-term goals.
   - **Investment Selection**: Use taxable accounts for investments that are relatively tax-efficient, such as index funds and ETFs, which tend to generate fewer capital gains.

### 3. **Asset Allocation**
   - Diversify your investments within each account type according to risk tolerance and time horizon. Generally, stocks are more suitable for long-term growth, while bonds may provide stability.

### 4. **Consider Withdrawal Needs**
   - Keep short-term needs in taxable accounts, while long-term growth can be targeted in tax-advantaged accounts where growth can compound without immediate tax implications.

### 5. **Rebalancing**
   - Regularly review your asset allocation and rebalance as necessary to maintain your investment strategy, especially as you approach retirement.

### Summary
Prioritize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts first, considering your tax situation, match opportunities, and investment goals. Use taxable accounts for flexibility and tax-efficient investments. Always reassess your strategy based on changes in your financial situation or goals.
