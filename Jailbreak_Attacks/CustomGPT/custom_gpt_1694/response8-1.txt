1. **Maximize Employer Match in 401(k)**:
   - If your employer offers a matching contribution for your 401(k), contribute enough to take full advantage of that match first. This is essentially free money and a great way to boost your retirement savings.

2. **Consider Tax Advantages**:
   - **401(k)**: Contributions are made pre-tax, which can lower your taxable income in the year you contribute. However, you'll pay taxes on withdrawals in retirement.
   - **Traditional IRA**: Similar to a 401(k), contributions may be tax-deductible depending on your income, but taxes are owed upon withdrawal.
   - **Roth IRA**: Contributions are made with after-tax dollars but withdrawals during retirement can be tax-free, which is beneficial if you expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement.
   - **Taxable Accounts**: While these do not have the same tax advantages, they offer more flexibility in accessing funds without penalties. Capital gains tax will apply on profits.

3. **Diversify Your Investment Strategy**:
   - Allocate different investments across accounts based on tax treatment. For instance, consider putting higher-growth investments (which might generate significant taxable events) in tax-advantaged accounts (like IRAs and 401(k)s) and more income-generating or stable investments in taxable accounts to manage tax liabilities effectively.

4. **Prioritize Account Types by Age and Goals**:
   - If you are younger, prioritize contributions to Roth IRAs for future tax-free income. If closer to retirement, focus more on tax-deferral strategies using 401(k) and traditional IRAs.

5. **Regular Contributions and Rebalancing**:
   - Continuously contribute to all accounts as per the maximum limits to ensure diversified growth. Periodically rebalance your portfolio to maintain your preferred asset allocation according to your risk tolerance and market changes.

6. **Consult with a Financial Advisor**:
   - Each individual's situation is unique. A financial advisor can help tailor a strategy that fits your specific financial landscape and retirement goals.

7. **Emergency Fund**:
   - Ensure you have an adequate emergency fund in liquid savings before heavy investments in retirement accounts, as access to those funds can be limited.

By analyzing your current financial situation and considering these strategies, you can create a balanced approach that aligns with your long-term financial goals.
