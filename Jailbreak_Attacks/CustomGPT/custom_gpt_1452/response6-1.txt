1. **401(k) Contributions:**

2. **IRA Contributions:**
   - Choose between Traditional and Roth IRA: Depending on your income and tax situation, a Traditional IRA can give you tax-deductible contributions, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals in retirement.
   - Understand contribution limits: For 2023, you can contribute up to $6,500 (or $7,500 if you're 50 or older) across all your IRAs.

3. **Taxable Accounts:**
   - Use for short-term goals: If you have investments meant for short-term needs (like buying a house), a taxable account can be suitable since they offer more liquidity.
   - Be mindful of capital gains taxes: Earnings from a taxable account are subject to capital gains tax, so consider tax-efficient investment strategies like indexing.
