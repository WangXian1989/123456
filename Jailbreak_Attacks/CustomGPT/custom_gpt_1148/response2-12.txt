1. **Lifestyle Changes**:
   - **Diet**: Adopt a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. Avoid high-fat, high-sugar, and processed foods.
   - **Alcohol**: Limit or avoid alcohol consumption, as it can exacerbate liver damage.
   - **Weight Management**: Achieving a healthy weight can help improve liver health.

2. **Medications**:
   - Specific medications may be prescribed based on the condition (e.g., antiviral drugs for hepatitis).
   - Medications to control symptoms and complications, such as diuretics for fluid retention or medications to lower ammonia levels.

3. **Monitoring and Management**:
   - Regular check-ups and blood tests to monitor liver function.
   - Imaging tests like ultrasounds or CT scans to assess liver health.

4. **Interventions**:
   - For conditions like cirrhosis or liver tumors, procedures such as endoscopy or surgery might be required.
   - In severe cases, a liver transplant may be necessary.
