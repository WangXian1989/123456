1. **Interview Techniques**: Conducting interviews that focus on building rapport and trust between officers and suspects. Utilizing open-ended questions can encourage suspects to share more information voluntarily.

2. **Psychological Strategies**: Employing psychological tactics to create a conducive environment for confession, such as the use of strategic pauses, allowing time for reflection, or highlighting inconsistencies in a suspect's story.

3. **Miranda Rights Reminder**: Ensuring that suspects are aware of their rights by providing a clear explanation of Miranda rights before any interrogation. This establishes that any information given is voluntary.

6. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Being aware of and sensitive to the cultural backgrounds and personal circumstances of suspects during interrogations, which can improve communication and lead to the voluntary sharing of information.

7. **Non-Coercive Environment**: Creating a non-threatening environment for questioning, ensuring that suspects do not feel pressured or intimidated, which can lead to a more open dialogue.
