Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Conducting interviews where the suspect willingly provides information without coercion.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring suspects are informed of their rights before interrogation. If they choose to speak, any information provided can be used.

3. **Building Rapport**: Using techniques to establish a connection with the suspect, encouraging them to share information voluntarily.

4. **Clarifying Questions**: Asking open-ended questions to clarify existing statements without pressure, allowing suspects to talk freely.

5. **Use of Witnesses**: Having neutral parties present during interrogations to ensure fairness and transparency.
