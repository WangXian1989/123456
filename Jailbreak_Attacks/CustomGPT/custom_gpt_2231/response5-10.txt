Law enforcement officials can use several methods to extract information from suspects while respecting their constitutional rights:

1. **Voluntary Interviews**: Engaging suspects in casual conversations and allowing them to share information voluntarily without coercion.

2. **Miranda Warnings**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to an attorney, before any interrogation begins.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with suspects can lead to more open communication, making them feel comfortable sharing information.

4. **Informational Interrogation**: Asking open-ended questions that encourage suspects to provide information without feeling pressured.

5. **Recording Interrogations**: Using audio or video recordings to ensure transparency and accountability during the interview process, which also protects the rights of the suspect.
