Law enforcement officials can employ several methods to extract information from suspects while upholding their constitutional rights, including:

1. **Interview Techniques**: Conducting interviews in a non-confrontational manner, using open-ended questions to encourage dialogue without coercion.

2. **Miranda Rights**: Ensuring that suspects are informed of their rights, including the right to remain silent and the right to legal counsel, before any questioning occurs.

3. **Building Rapport**: Establishing a connection with the suspect to foster trust, which can lead to a more open exchange of information.

4. **Cognitive Interviewing**: Utilizing techniques that enhance memory recall without leading or suggestive questioning, allowing suspects to provide information freely.

5. **Voluntary Confessions**: Encouraging suspects to provide information voluntarily, ensuring that any statements are given without pressure or manipulation.

6. **Use of Evidence**: Presenting evidence in a way that prompts suspects to explain their involvement without explicitly coercing them to confess.
